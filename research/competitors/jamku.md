# Jamku - Deep Dive

## Overview
- **Website:** https://jamku.app
- **Category:** Practice Management
- **Founded:** 2013
- **HQ:** Mumbai, Maharashtra
- **Company:** Jamku
- **Funding:** Bootstrapped
- **Team Size:** ~5-10
- **Users/Customers:** 10,000+ professionals

## Product Description
Jamku is the oldest dedicated CA practice management system in India, operating since 2013. It offers a freemium model with free access for teams of fewer than 3 users, which has helped it build a base of 10,000+ professionals over its decade-plus history. The platform covers core PM needs including task management, client management, and compliance tracking.

Jamku has an interesting product architecture -- the core PMS is one product (Jamku App), while Jamku Portal is a SEPARATE product that handles IT, GST, and TDS auto-fetch from government portals. This split means users wanting both PM and compliance automation need to purchase two products, adding friction and cost.

Despite its longevity and substantial user base, Jamku suffers from a dated UI and has not kept pace with modern UX standards. Its cross-platform availability (web, Windows, Mac, mobile) is a strength, but the design feels like a product from 2013 that has been incrementally updated rather than redesigned.

## Target Segment
- Solo practitioners and small firms (free tier)
- Mid-size CA firms (paid tiers)
- Long-time users loyal to the platform
- CAs wanting cross-platform access

## Integrations
- Government portals (via Jamku Portal -- separate product)
  - Income Tax portal
  - GST portal
  - TDS portal
- No Tally integration
- No WhatsApp integration
- No payment gateway integration

## Strengths
- Oldest Indian CA PMS (since 2013) -- maximum battle-testing
- 10,000+ professional user base
- Free tier for small teams (<3 users)
- Cross-platform availability
- Decade of domain knowledge
- Government portal integration (via Jamku Portal)

## Weaknesses & Gaps
- Dated user interface -- looks like a 2013 product
- Compliance auto-fetch is a SEPARATE product (Jamku Portal)
- No Tally integration
- No WhatsApp integration
- No payment gateway
- No client-facing portal
- No AI or modern automation features
- Small team relative to product age (not scaling)
- Innovation has stagnated
- No billing or invoicing
- Split product architecture adds friction

## Unique Selling Points
- Oldest Indian CA PMS with longest track record
- Free for teams under 3 users
- Cross-platform (rare among Indian PMS tools)
- Government portal auto-fetch via Jamku Portal

## Threat Assessment for CAOS
- **Threat Level:** MEDIUM
- **Where they compete:** Core practice management, free tier for small teams, government portal integration (via separate product), established user base of 10K+ professionals.
- **Where CAOS can win:** Modern UX (Jamku looks dated), all-in-one platform (no separate portal product needed), WhatsApp integration, Tally integration, payment collection, AI automation, client portal. Jamku's 10K+ users represent a large pool of professionals who are familiar with PM tools but likely frustrated with the dated experience -- prime conversion targets for CAOS.
