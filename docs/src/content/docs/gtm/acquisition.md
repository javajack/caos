## Acquisition Strategy Overview

CAOS acquires customers through a mix of community-driven, content-driven, and paid channels. The channel mix shifts over time as the product matures and brand awareness grows.

| Channel Category | Channels | % of Acquisition (Mature) | Blended CAC |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Community** | ICAI events, WhatsApp groups, CA portals | 25-30% | INR 1,000-2,500 |
| **Referral** | CA-to-CA referral program | 25-35% | INR 500-1,500 |
| **Content/Inbound** | SEO, YouTube, LinkedIn organic | 15-20% | INR 800-2,000 |
| **Paid** | Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads | 15-25% | INR 2,500-5,000 |
| **Direct sales** | Outbound for enterprise | 5-10% | INR 5,000-15,000 |

:::note
The target channel mix intentionally weights community and referral channels at 50-65% of total acquisition. This keeps blended CAC low and acquisition quality high. Paid channels supplement but never dominate.
:::

## Channel 1: ICAI Branch Events

### Channel Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Channel type | Community / Event |
| Reach | 160+ ICAI branches, 50-200 CAs per event |
| CAC range | INR 1,000-3,000 |
| Conversion timeline | 1-4 weeks from event to signup |
| Quality | HIGH -- engaged, motivated CAs |
| Scalability | MEDIUM -- limited by event calendar and geography |

### Execution Playbook

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Identify target branches | Top 20 cities by CA density: Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune, Ahmedabad, Kolkata, Jaipur, Lucknow | M1 |
| 2. Contact branch committees | Propose co-sponsored CPE seminar on "Practice Management in the Digital Age" | M1-2 |
| 3. Prepare event kit | Demo script, trial vouchers, feedback forms, brochures | M2 |
| 4. Execute events | 15-minute live demo + Q&A + distribute trial codes | M2-6 |
| 5. Post-event nurture | WhatsApp follow-up within 24 hours, onboarding support within 72 hours | Ongoing |
| 6. Track and optimize | Measure signups and activation per event, double down on high-conversion branches | Monthly |

### Expected Funnel

| Stage | Conversion | Count (per event of 100 attendees) |
|---|---|---|
| Attend event | -- | 100 |
| Show interest (scan QR / take voucher) | 60% | 60 |
| Register on CAOS | 40% | 24 |
| Activate (upload first document) | 50% | 12 |
| Convert to paid (within 6 months) | 15% | 1.8 |

**Cost per event:** INR 25,000-50,000
**Paid customers per event:** ~2
**Effective CAC:** INR 14,000-28,000 per paid customer (but INR 1,000-2,000 per registered user, many of whom convert later)

:::tip
ICAI events are not primarily a paid conversion channel. They are a trust-building and activation channel. The CAs acquired at events have higher activation rates and lower churn than any other channel because they have seen the product live and had their questions answered in person.
:::

### Top CA YouTube Channels (Indicative)

| Channel Category | Subscriber Range | Content Focus | Collaboration Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| CA exam + practice | 200K-500K | Exam prep, career advice, practice tips | Sponsored video + mentions |
| Tax and compliance | 100K-300K | GST, ITR, tax planning | Product demo integration |
| CA practice management | 50K-150K | Tools, productivity, firm management | Deep partnership, co-creation |
| Finance and business | 300K-1M | Broader audience, some CA overlap | Brand awareness, wider reach |

### Collaboration Models

| Model | Cost | Expected Signups | CAC |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dedicated review video (15 min) | INR 100K-300K | 200-800 | INR 375-1,500 |
| Integrated mention (2-3 min segment) | INR 30K-80K | 50-200 | INR 400-1,600 |
| Long-term ambassador (monthly content) | INR 50K-150K/mo | 100-400/mo | INR 375-1,500 |
| Affiliate (pay per signup) | INR 200-500/signup | Variable | INR 200-500 |

### Content Strategy for Influencer Videos

Every influencer video should follow this structure:
1. **Pain point hook** (30 sec): "How much time do you spend sorting client documents?"
2. **Product demo** (5-8 min): Live walkthrough of document upload, classification, extraction
3. **ROI proof** (2 min): Show time savings calculation with real numbers
4. **Call to action** (30 sec): "Sign up free at caos.app -- link in description"

:::caution
Influencer marketing in the CA space requires authenticity. CAs are skeptical of overt advertising. The best-performing collaborations are those where the influencer has genuinely used the product and speaks from experience. Avoid scripted promotional content.
:::

| Quarter | % of Annual Budget | Rationale |

:::note

## Channel 5: WhatsApp Groups

### Channel Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Channel type | Community / Organic |
| Reach | Thousands of CA WhatsApp groups across India |
| CAC range | INR 500-2,000 |
| Conversion timeline | Variable -- days to months |
| Quality | MEDIUM -- context-dependent |
| Scalability | MEDIUM -- labor-intensive, risk of being seen as spam |

### Execution Guidelines

| Do | Do Not |
|---|---|
| Share genuinely useful content (regulatory updates, tips) | Spam groups with promotional messages |
| Answer questions and provide value before mentioning CAOS | Post unsolicited product links |
| Share case studies and ROI data when relevant | Create fake accounts or astroturf |
| Respect group admin rules | Post in groups without admin permission |
| Create CAOS-specific groups for interested CAs | Violate WhatsApp's commercial messaging policies |

### WhatsApp Funnel

| Stage | Action | Expected Conversion |
|---|---|---|
| Group member sees content | Useful regulatory update or tip from CAOS | -- |
| Engages (reply, question) | Shows interest in the topic | 5-10% |
| Visits CAOS (link in profile/message) | Clicks through to learn more | 20-30% of engaged |
| Registers | Signs up for free tier | 30-40% of visitors |
| Activates | Uploads first document | 40-50% of registered |

## Channel 7: Referral Program

### Channel Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Channel type | Viral / Word-of-mouth |
| Reach | Every existing user is a potential referral source |
| CAC range | INR 500-1,500 |
| Conversion timeline | 1-2 weeks from referral to signup |
| Quality | VERY HIGH -- trust-based acquisition |
| Scalability | HIGH -- scales with user base |

### Referral Program Design

| Tier | Trigger | Referrer Reward | Referee Reward |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic | First referral | 1 month free on current plan | 1 month free Professional |
| Silver | 5 referrals | 3 months free on current plan | 1 month free Professional |
| Gold | 10 referrals | 6 months free on current plan | 2 months free Professional |
| Ambassador | 25+ referrals | Lifetime Professional tier | 3 months free Professional |

### Referral Mechanics

| Feature | Implementation |
|---|---|
| Unique referral link | Generated per user, tracked via analytics |
| In-app referral prompt | "Know a CA who needs this? Share and both get 1 month free" |
| WhatsApp share button | One-tap share to WhatsApp with pre-filled message |
| Referral dashboard | Track referrals, rewards earned, pending conversions |
| Automated reward fulfillment | Credits applied automatically when referee activates |

:::tip
The referral program must be embarrassingly easy to use. One tap to share via WhatsApp, auto-filled message with a compelling value proposition, and automatic reward fulfillment. Every friction point in the referral flow halves the referral rate.
:::

## Summary

Customer acquisition for CAOS follows the professional community playbook: trust-first channels (ICAI events, influencers, referrals) for the first 12 months, supplemented by paid channels (Google, LinkedIn) calibrated to seasonal demand. The target blended CAC of INR 2,000-4,000 is achievable through heavy weighting toward community and referral channels (50-65% of acquisition). Seasonal optimization around ITR filing season (July-September) concentrates paid spend where intent is highest. The referral program is the single most important growth lever after the product itself.
