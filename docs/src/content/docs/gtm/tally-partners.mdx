Tally Solutions runs the largest single distribution channel for accounting and CA-adjacent software in India. CAOS positions as a layer **above** Tally (see [Tally / ERP integration](/caos/product/tally-erp-integration/)), which makes the Tally partner network a natural reseller rail rather than a competitor channel. This page covers (1) the verified scale of the Tally partner ecosystem, (2) why Master partners are the right Phase-1 pilot, (3) the economics of the partnership, and (4) the 20-Master target for Year 1.

The two-tier structure is the lever. **Master partners** are the ~140 highest-tier resellers — typically 5-Star Certified, with their own multi-city presence, dedicated training capacity, and direct relationships with Tally HQ in Bangalore. They are themselves businesses with ₹5-50 cr+ annual revenue from Tally licensing + customization + customer support. **Secondary partners** (~18,000) sub-distribute through Master partners, run small reseller offices, and are typically owner-operated.

## Why Master partners are the right Phase-1 pilot

<Aside type="tip" title="The compression argument">
A Master partner already has the trust of 200-2,000 Tally-using businesses in their region, plus the technical know-how to deploy software, plus the customer-support muscle to handle integration questions. CAOS gets all three for the cost of a revenue-share — none of which any direct sales team can match for the same investment.
</Aside>

Three structural reasons Master partners win the pilot:

1. **They are pre-trusted by the buyer**. The CA firm or SMB-finance team that buys Tally already trusts their Master partner for license decisions, customization, and upgrades. CAOS as a "Tally companion app from your trusted Tally partner" lands warm, not cold.

2. **They have the technical capacity to deploy**. Master partners deploy Tally TDLs (custom modules), train staff, configure GST settings, troubleshoot Tally-CAOS data flows. CAOS doesn't need its own field-deploy team in every Tier-2 city — the Master partner has one already.

3. **They have the regional density CAOS lacks**. ICAI's [West-Central-South-North-East distribution is 32-21-20-19-8%](/caos/stakeholders/practitioners/), and CAOS will struggle to be in 25 cities at launch. Master partners give instant cluster reach in their region — typically 5-15 cities each.

## The economics

A Master-partner pilot has three economic layers:

| Layer | Master partner gets | CAOS gets |
|---|---|---|
| **License resale** | 25-30% of first-year ARR; 15-20% recurring | 70-75% of first-year ARR; 80-85% recurring |
| **Implementation fee** | 100% of one-time setup fee (₹5K-50K per client) | 0 |
| **Annual support contract** | 70% of annual support fee; CAOS provides L2/L3 escalation | 30% of support fee + L2/L3 brand control |
| **Upsell to Business SKU** | 15% of upsell ARR (Business SKU — see [Three SKUs](/caos/business-model/three-skus/)) | 85% of upsell ARR |

The numbers are anchored to standard Indian channel-software economics. A typical Master partner running 20-50 client deployments per year of CAOS at average ₹1.2 lakh/year ARR generates ₹6-15 lakh/year of partner income on the licensing line alone, before implementation and support.

CAOS CAC via this channel is the partner-program management overhead (small, fixed) divided by partner-driven ARR (variable, growing). At 20 Master partners × 30 deals/year × ₹1.2L ARR = ₹720 lakh ARR / year through the channel; partner-program staff cost ~₹40-60 lakh/year. CAC per ₹1 of ARR is roughly ₹0.06-0.08 — a structural advantage over direct field sales (CAC typically ₹0.30-0.60 per ₹1 of ARR for Indian B2B SaaS at this ARPU).

## The 20-Master target

Year 1 target: **20 Master partners onboarded by Month 12**, generating 600 client deployments by Month 18.

The ramp:

| Quarter | Master partners onboarded | Cumulative deals | Cumulative ARR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | 3 (founding pilot) | 30 | ₹35 lakh |
| Q2 | +5 (8 cumulative) | 120 | ₹1.4 cr |
| Q3 | +6 (14 cumulative) | 280 | ₹3.4 cr |
| Q4 | +6 (20 cumulative) | 600 | ₹7.2 cr |

This single channel could drive ~₹7 cr of Year 1 ARR alone, before any other GTM motion (CA-referral, direct, ICAI MOU). It also generates the deepest Tally-CAOS integration feedback loop — Master partners surface every edge case in the first 90 days.

## What CAOS must give the Master partner

For the pilot to work, CAOS commits to:

1. **Co-branded marketing kit**: deck, demo videos, case studies (after first 5 deployments), webinar templates — all customizable with the Master partner's logo and contact info.
2. **Partner portal**: deal registration, lead routing, commission tracking, certification courses (3-day training + exam), MDF requests.
3. **Dedicated partner manager** for the first 6 months — single point of contact for every Master partner.
4. **L2/L3 technical escalation**: SLA-bound (8-hour response, 48-hour resolution for P1 issues) so the Master partner is never embarrassed in front of a client.
5. **Tally TDL compatibility certification** for every CAOS release — the Master partner's customers can't have Tally crashes from CAOS integrations.
6. **Quarterly business review**: pipeline, conversion rate, NPS, share of wallet, pricing experiments.

## Risks specific to this channel

<Aside type="caution" title="Channel-specific failure modes">
- **Tally Solutions builds a competing product** (TallyEdge, TallyCapital adjacencies; see [Counterfactual scenarios](/caos/risks/counterfactual/)). Mitigation: lock in 20 Master partners with 3-year commitments before Tally HQ ships a rival.
- **Master partner becomes captive to the highest-paying SaaS partner** in their portfolio. Mitigation: 3-year exclusive on CAOS in their region in exchange for accelerated rev-share tier.
- **Channel conflict** with direct CA-referral channel where the same client is touched by both. Mitigation: deal-registration system with 30-day exclusivity for whoever registered first.
- **Master partner sells discounted licenses to win deals**. Mitigation: floor pricing with margin protection; partner agreements specify minimum sell price per SKU.
</Aside>

## How this connects to the rest of the flywheel

The Tally Master partner channel is one of three Year-1 channels that drive [Phase 1 — CA-Led Practitioner Network](/caos/flywheel/phase-1-ca-led/) ignition. The other two are direct CA-referral ([CA referral motion](/caos/gtm/ca-referral/)) and ICAI branch-level MOUs (covered in [Channel architecture](/caos/gtm/channel-architecture/)). All three should run in parallel — the Tally channel reaches CAs and finance teams already on Tally; CA-referral reaches CA firms across all accounting platforms; ICAI MOU reaches new CAs joining the profession. They cover the same buyer pool from different angles.

Tally Master partners are also the Phase-1 path to the **vendor-network flywheel** ([Phase 2](/caos/flywheel/phase-2-vendor-network/)) — every Tally-using SMB has a vendor list inside Tally already. CAOS can extract that vendor list (with consent) and use it to bootstrap the vendor-network graph. The Master partner channel doesn't just deliver clients — it delivers each client's vendor universe pre-attached.

See [Sequencing](/caos/gtm/sequencing/) for the 60-month picture of how this channel evolves into Year 2 (vendor-network expansion via Tally data) and Year 3 (Master partners as Audit Workspace co-deployers with mid-tier audit firms).
