India's Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) — Account Aggregator framework, GST Suvidha Provider ecosystem, Income Tax e-Return Intermediary registry, DigiLocker, eSign, UDIN, and the eMudhra-led DSC certifying-authority network — is simultaneously the substrate that the Phase-4 fact-graph runs on AND the regulatory moat that prevents 2027-vintage competitors from showing up overnight. CAOS participates as a partner in Year 2 and as a registered entity in Year 3. This page covers the participation path for each ecosystem and the verified-data anchors that justify the timing.

## The participation strategy

<Aside type="tip" title="The choice">
**Become an FIU + ASP-first (under existing GSP) in Year 2; Type 2 ERI in Year 3.** This is the recommendation from [Decision D7](/caos/decisions/d1-d7/). The reasoning: light registrations (FIU, ASP-under-GSP) cost weeks; full registrations (own GSP licence at ₹5-10 cr deposit, ERI Type 2 with ITD-approved infra) cost months. Sequence by ROI per regulatory dollar.
</Aside>

The ecosystem participation choice is not "all in" or "stay out." It is a sequenced layering — partner first, register later, build last.

CAOS's path through the AA ecosystem:

1. **Year 1 — passive consumer**. CAOS uses one of the 17 NBFC-AAs (likely OneMoney / Finvu / CAMSFinServ / Anumati-Perfios) via a Technology Service Provider (TSP) integration. No registration needed; CAOS is one of many downstream tools using the data.

2. **Year 2 — FIU registration**. CAOS registers as a Financial Information User with one or more AAs. This makes CAOS a first-class consent-receiver and gives the brand AA-stack legitimacy. FIU registration is light (weeks, not months) and free at the AA level (each AA has its own onboarding).

3. **Year 3 — TSP partnership for downstream redistribution**. CAOS can offer its own customers (audit firms, CFOs) AA-mediated bank-statement access *as a CAOS feature*, not just an integration. This requires partnering with a TSP (or building one) and signing data-redistribution agreements with AAs. The market is already proven: 22.3M monthly consents shows the rails work at scale.

4. **Year 4+ — own AA?** Optional and probably not needed. Becoming an NBFC-AA requires ~₹2 cr net worth + RBI licence + ongoing compliance — only justified if CAOS needs *consent-orchestration* control beyond what TSPs offer. Most likely outcome: stay as FIU + TSP partner, never become an AA.

The AA ecosystem is the data substrate for the [Phase-4 fact graph](/caos/flywheel/phase-4-dpi-graph/). Every CAOS enterprise customer with AA-connected bank accounts becomes an underwriting-ready node — banks/NBFCs/lenders pay for graph access, CAOS pays the AA per consent.

CAOS's path:

1. **Year 1-2 — ASP under existing GSP**. CAOS partners with a Tier-1 GSP (Cygnet, IRIS, Mastersindia, Webtel) as an Application Service Provider. The GSP holds the regulatory relationship; CAOS builds the application layer. Cygnet's "First ASP-GSP with IMS" template is the model — CAOS can do the same as a partner.

2. **Year 3 — multi-GSP redundancy**. With volume to justify, CAOS maintains relationships with 2-3 GSPs for redundancy. If one GSP has API issues, traffic routes to another. This is operationally expensive but eliminates single-vendor risk.

3. **Year 4+ — own GSP?** Likely yes once CAOS hits ~10K active enterprise customers using GST APIs. The ₹5-10 cr deposit becomes a rounding error against the ARR generated, and full GSP status removes the partner economics drag.

CAOS's path:

1. **Year 1-2 — partner with existing ERI**. ClearTax is an ERI. So is TaxCloud. So are several smaller players. CAOS can integrate with their APIs.

2. **Year 3 — Type 2 ERI registration**. With the Bank Guarantee + audit infrastructure in place (as part of SOC 2 Type II buildout for [audit firm partnerships](/caos/gtm/audit-firm-partnerships/)), Type 2 ERI is a 90-day filing process. CAOS then runs its own ITR filing pipeline, owns the relationship with CBDT, and removes the ERI-partner dependency for income tax filings.

CAOS's path:

1. **Become an authorised eMudhra DSC reseller** (Year 1, easy): standard partnership; 5-15% margin per DSC sold; gives CAOS a bundled DSC purchase flow inside the product (CFO gets DSC + CAOS in one onboarding).

2. **DSC management layer** (Year 1-2): even without reseller status, CAOS can offer DSC inventory management ("which director / authorised signatory across 50 subsidiaries holds which DSC, with expiry tracking and e-sign integration"). This is one of the an underclaimed surface area — eMudhra and competitors don't ship this; CAOS can.

3. **e-Sign integration** (Year 1-2): partner with Signzy (500+ customers), Leegality (5,000+ businesses), or build direct integration with eMudhra's eSign API. The volume is real; UIDAI's Aadhaar-eSign infrastructure handles millions of signatures monthly.

## Why this is a moat, not just a feature

<Aside type="tip" title="The regulatory moat compounds">
A 2027-vintage competitor that wants to compete with CAOS on the same flywheel must also acquire FIU + ASP + ERI Type 2 + eMudhra reseller + eSign relationships. That takes 18-24 months minimum and requires regulatory acceptance that's not always given. **CAOS that starts these registrations in Year 1-3 has a 24-month moat by Year 4.**

This is the single biggest reason DPI participation belongs in GTM, not just engineering. The participation IS the distribution.
</Aside>

Each registration also carries a brand-credibility halo. CAOS as "FIU-registered + Type 2 ERI + Cygnet GSP partner + eMudhra reseller" is a much harder sell to displace than CAOS as "yet another SaaS app." Enterprise procurement teams and CIOs evaluate vendor stacks; DPI participation is a checkbox they expect.

## Connection to other channels

DPI partnerships **multiply** the [audit firm channel](/caos/gtm/audit-firm-partnerships/) — audit firms increasingly want their tooling stack to be DPI-native (NFRA scrutiny is moving in that direction). They **strengthen** the [Tally Master partner pilot](/caos/gtm/tally-partners/) — Tally Master partners can sell CAOS as "the DPI-ready layer above Tally" rather than just an add-on. They **enable** the [vendor-network flywheel](/caos/flywheel/phase-2-vendor-network/) at scale because vendor verification becomes consent-driven via AA, not document-collection-driven.
