---

The Indian CA profession operates on a relentless compliance calendar. Unlike many professional services where workload ebbs and flows with market conditions, CA firms face mandatory, government-imposed deadlines every single month. Understanding this calendar is essential for product design, go-to-market timing, and user engagement strategy.

:::tip
The single most important insight about CA firm seasonality: **there is no off-season**. Every month has at least one major deadline, and most months have multiple overlapping obligations across different compliance domains. This creates both pain (burnout, deadline pressure, client anxiety) and opportunity (daily engagement with any tool that helps manage the chaos).
:::

## GST Monthly Cycle

GST compliance is the single most frequent recurring obligation, requiring action from almost every CA firm every single month.

### Monthly GST Filing Calendar

| Date | Filing | Description | Applicability |
|---|---|---|---|
| 11th | GSTR-1 | Outward supply details | All regular taxpayers |
| 13th | GSTR-1 (QRMP) | Quarterly filers (IFF for M1, M2) | Turnover up to INR 5 Cr |
| 13th | GSTR-5 | Non-resident taxable persons | NRI businesses |
| 20th | GSTR-3B | Monthly summary return with tax payment | All regular taxpayers |
| 22nd/24th | GSTR-3B (QRMP) | Quarterly filers (state-wise staggered dates) | Turnover up to INR 5 Cr |
| 25th | PMT-06 | Monthly tax payment for QRMP filers | QRMP scheme |
| 28th | GSTR-11 | UIN holders (embassies, UN bodies) | UIN holders |
| Last day | ITC reconciliation | GSTR-2B matching with purchase records | All taxpayers |

### GST Annual/Periodic Filings

| Filing | Due Date | Description |
|---|---|---|
| GSTR-9 (Annual Return) | Dec 31 | Annual consolidated return |
| GSTR-9C (Reconciliation Statement) | Dec 31 | Audit reconciliation (turnover > INR 5 Cr) |
| GSTR-4 (Composition Annual) | Apr 30 | Annual return for composition dealers |
| GST audit | Dec 31 | Self-certification with GSTR-9C |

:::note
A typical CA firm handling 200 GST clients files approximately 2,400 GST returns per year (12 months x 200 clients). At even 30 minutes per return for preparation, review, and filing, this represents 1,200 person-hours annually -- over half a full-time employee dedicated purely to GST return filing. Automation of data collection, reconciliation, and filing can save 40-60% of this time.
:::

## TDS Quarterly Cycle

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) compliance follows a quarterly rhythm with monthly deposit obligations.

### TDS Monthly Deposits

| Payment Date | For Month | Applicability |
|---|---|---|
| 7th of following month | Previous month's deductions | All deductors |
| 30th April | March deductions | Government deductors (different timeline) |

### TDS Quarterly Return Filing

| Quarter | Period | Due Date | Form |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | April - June | July 31 | 24Q, 26Q, 27Q |
| Q2 | July - September | October 31 | 24Q, 26Q, 27Q |
| Q3 | October - December | January 31 | 24Q, 26Q, 27Q |
| Q4 | January - March | May 31 | 24Q, 26Q, 27Q |

### TDS Forms by Type

| Form | Coverage | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| 24Q | Salary TDS | Quarterly |
| 26Q | Non-salary TDS (rent, professional fees, etc.) | Quarterly |
| 27Q | TDS on non-resident payments | Quarterly |
| 27EQ | TCS (Tax Collected at Source) | Quarterly |
| Form 16 | Employee TDS certificate | Annual (due June 15) |
| Form 16A | Non-salary TDS certificate | Quarterly (within 15 days of return) |

:::caution
TDS defaults attract severe penalties: INR 200/day for late filing (Section 234E), plus potential prosecution. Many small firms handle TDS for 50-200 employer clients, making the quarterly filing cycle a high-stakes, high-stress period. A missed TDS return can result in penalties that exceed the CA's entire fee for the service.
:::

## Income Tax Annual Cycle

### Key ITR Deadlines

| Category | Deadline | Volume Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Individuals/HUFs (no audit) | July 31 | Highest volume; 5+ crore filings |
| Businesses requiring audit | October 31 | High complexity; requires audit report first |
| Transfer pricing cases | November 30 | Specialized; limited volume |
| Belated/revised returns | December 31 | Catch-up filings |
| Updated returns (ITR-U) | 24 months from AY end | New provision; growing use |

### The July Rush

July is the single most intense month for most CA firms. The July 31 ITR deadline drives a surge in activity:

| Week | Activity | Stress Level |
|---|---|---|
| July 1-10 | Document collection, client follow-up, data entry begins | Medium |
| July 11-20 | Bulk ITR preparation, initial filings, complex case review | High |
| July 21-25 | Filing acceleration, client panic calls, missing documents | Very High |
| July 26-30 | All-hands filing, late-night sessions, portal overload | Extreme |
| July 31 | Filing deadline; portal crashes common; extension requests | Critical |

:::note
The income tax e-filing portal historically experiences performance degradation or outages in the final days before the July 31 deadline. In 2023, the portal was partially inaccessible on July 31, leading to deadline extensions. CA firms must plan for portal unreliability during peak filing periods.
:::

### Advance Tax Schedule

| Installment | Due Date | Cumulative % of Tax Liability |
|---|---|---|
| 1st installment | June 15 | 15% |
| 2nd installment | September 15 | 45% |
| 3rd installment | December 15 | 75% |
| 4th installment | March 15 | 100% |

Advance tax calculation and client communication creates a mini-cycle four times a year. Each installment requires the CA to estimate the client's current-year income, calculate the advance tax liability, and communicate the payment amount -- often for hundreds of clients simultaneously.

## Audit Season

### Statutory Audit Timeline

| Phase | Timing | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| Planning | April - June | Client list finalization, engagement letters, team assignment |
| Fieldwork | July - September | On-site audits, vouching, verification, management queries |
| Reporting | September - October | Audit report drafting, CARO reporting, management letter |
| Filing | October 31 | Tax audit report upload, ITR for audit cases |

### Tax Audit (Section 44AB) Key Dates

| Milestone | Date | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Audit appointment | Before September 1 (recommended) | Engagement letter, fee negotiation |
| Audit completion | By September 25 | Fieldwork and report finalization |
| Audit report upload | September 30 | Upload to IT portal |
| ITR filing for audit cases | October 31 | File ITR for audited entities |

:::tip
Audit season (July-October) is when CA firms are most overwhelmed and most likely to seek efficiency tools. However, it is also the worst time to ask them to learn new software. The optimal window for product onboarding is **February-June** -- busy enough that pain points are fresh, but not so busy that learning is impossible.
:::

## Implications for Product Strategy

### Feature Prioritization by Season

| Season | Priority Features | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| April-June (Pre-season) | Onboarding, document collection, client setup | Firms are planning; best time for adoption |
| July-October (Peak) | Bulk filing, deadline tracking, status dashboards | Survival mode; efficiency is paramount |
| November-January (Wrap-up) | Reconciliation, annual returns, billing | Closing out the year; collections focus |
| February-March (Planning) | Tax planning, advisory tools, renewal | Strategic work; upsell opportunity |

### Marketing and Sales Calendar

| Window | Approach | Message |
|---|---|---|
| January-March | Product demos, free trials | "Get ready for the next filing season" |
| April-May | Onboarding surge, CPE seminars | "Start the new FY organized" |
| July-September | Avoid cold outreach; focus on existing users | "We're in the trenches with you" |
| October-November | Case studies, ROI messaging | "Look how much time you saved this season" |

:::caution
Never launch a major product update or require migration during July-October. Firms will not have bandwidth to learn new features, and any disruption during peak filing season will generate disproportionate negative sentiment. Save major releases for February-April.
:::

## The Compounding Problem

The critical insight about CA firm seasonality is not any individual deadline -- it is the compound effect of overlapping obligations:

- A firm with 200 GST clients and 500 ITR clients faces, in July alone: 200 GSTR-1 filings, 200 GSTR-3B filings, 500 ITR filings, TDS Q1 returns for 100 employer clients, and ongoing document collection for audit engagements
- That is potentially 1,000+ discrete compliance tasks in a single month, with hard legal deadlines and financial penalties for default
- Without systematic tracking, things fall through the cracks -- and when they do, it is the CA's reputation and client relationship at stake

This compounding effect is what makes practice management software not a luxury but a necessity for any growing firm. The calendar does not get lighter. Each year brings new compliance requirements, new forms, new portals. The only sustainable response is systematic, software-enabled workflow management.
