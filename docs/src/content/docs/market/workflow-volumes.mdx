The [market overview](/caos/market/overview/) sized the *population* of buyers; the [TAM sizing](/caos/market/tam-sizing/) sized the *addressable spend*; the [regulatory tailwinds](/caos/market/regulatory-tailwinds/) sized the *demand-creation rate*. This page sizes the **throughput** — the volume of documents, returns, notices, consents, and filings that flow through India's compliance pipes every year.

Throughput is the load-bearing input for CAOS's per-volume pricing model and for the AI-extraction cost economics ([P1 + P14 primitives](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/)). At 22.31 million AA consents per month, 13.84 cr e-way bills per month, 1.2 cr GSTR-1 filers per month, and ~1.7 cr UDINs per year, the per-document unit economics dominate the per-firm subscription economics on the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) Phase 4 ceiling.

Every number on this page is cited to a regulator, regulator-published dashboard, named industry body, or named press citing one.

### What this means for CAOS

- **1.2+ cr GSTR-1 filers/month × 12** = ~14.4 cr GSTR-1 returns per year, plus matched GSTR-3B and 2B. That is the throughput of the [P7 reconciliation engine](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/) at scale.
- **4.2 lakh+ notices/year** is the demand surface for the [P6 notice-lifecycle primitive](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/). At an average ₹15-50K/notice for representation and reply, this is a ₹600-2,000 cr legal-services market that CAOS slot into as the tooling layer.
- **₹22.08 lakh cr in GST collection** is the financial-flow scale of the verified-active business population. Even routing 0.01% of that flow through CAOS as decision-quality data = ₹2,200 cr touched.
- **GSTAT operational from Sep 2025** means appeals can finally move; the backlog of disputed assessments is now active demand for [P5 (evidence locker) + P6 (notice lifecycle)](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/).

### What this means for CAOS

- **29.5 lakh tax audit reports** is the upper-bound on Form 3CA-CD / 3CB-CD volume. At 1,59,557 COP-holding CAs, that's ~18 audits per CA per year on average — a meaningful fraction of practitioner billable time and the throughput of the [P14 AI-extraction-over-Indian-semi-structured-forms primitive](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/).
- **7.28 cr ITRs** is the throughput of the [P15 schedule generator](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/) at the upper bound. CAOS sells the *enterprise / corporate* slice of this, not retail ITR — but the CBDT infrastructure (Form 26AS, AIS, TIS feeds) that CAOS reads is the same.
- **₹4.76 lakh cr in refunds** is the working-capital scale of refund-tracking workflows. Faster refund tracking (P5 + P15) is a discrete value proposition for in-house tax leads.

This is the document factory CAOS connects to. The total surface — GST + IT + MCA + AA + DigiLocker + UDIN + EPFO/ESIC + e-way bills + DSCs — is in the **billions of digital artefacts per year**, growing at 30-80% YoY across multiple pipes.

Three implications:

1. **Per-document unit economics dominate at scale.** CAOS's pricing model needs both subscription and per-volume tiers; full per-volume on the AI extraction side ([P1 + P14](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/)) is the only way to keep gross margins > 60% as the throughput grows 30-80% YoY.

2. **The DPI pipes (AA + DigiLocker + UDIN) are the fastest-growing.** AA at +78.6% YoY consent growth is the single fastest signal in the entire compliance economy — the [Phase 4 DPI fact-graph](/caos/flywheel/composite/) is sized against this growth curve.

3. **The throughput is enough to support multiple winners.** No single platform — including Tally (₹710 cr FY25), ClearTax (~₹165 cr revenue), Vyapar (₹130 cr ARR), or any Big-4 — touches more than a small fraction of this throughput today. The market is structurally underbuilt; CAOS's flywheel is sized to capture a meaningful share, not all of it.
