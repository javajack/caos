The CAOS thesis is not that CAOS will *create* demand — it is that **regulation is creating demand faster than incumbents can ship**, and CAOS's job is to ride five simultaneous waves. Each of the five tailwinds covered on this page is independent: collapse in any one does not threaten the others, and the regulatory probability of any single one *reversing* (vs slowing) is structurally low because each has been written into either parliamentary law or a SEBI / RBI / CBDT / MCA notification with multi-year glide paths.

This page makes the five waves concrete with verified dates, thresholds, and implications.

## Wave 1 — DPI maturity (Account Aggregator + DigiLocker + UDIN)

Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) is the Indian government's umbrella term for the consent-driven data-sharing rails — Aadhaar, UPI, Account Aggregator, DigiLocker, OCEN, Bharat KYC. The two that matter most for CAOS are **Account Aggregator (AA)** for live financial data flows and **DigiLocker** for verified document custody.

## Wave 2 — E-invoice ratchet (the most extreme regulatory expansion)

This is the single fastest example of regulatory expansion narrowing the addressable buyer base — from ~700 cos when it started to **9.92 lakh active generators** today, in five years.

### The threshold timeline

| Phase | AATO threshold | Effective | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | ₹500 cr | Oct 1, 2020 | [Tax2win](https://tax2win.in/guide/e-invoicing-gst), [Sovos](https://sovos.com/vat/tax-rules/e-invoicing-india/) |
| 2 | ₹100 cr | Jan 1, 2021 | (same) |
| 3 | ₹50 cr | Apr 1, 2021 | (same) |
| 4 | ₹20 cr | Apr 1, 2022 | (same) |
| 5 | ₹10 cr | Oct 1, 2022 | (same) |
| 6 | **₹5 cr** | Aug 1, 2023 | [ClearTax](https://cleartax.in/s/e-invoicing-businesses-above-rs-5-crore-turnover) |
| Reporting time-limit (AATO ≥ ₹10 cr) | 30 days from invoice date | Apr 1, 2025 | [Tax2win](https://tax2win.in/guide/e-invoicing-gst), [Bajaj Finserv](https://www.bajajfinserv.in/e-invoice-limit) |

### The verified active population

| Metric | Value | As of | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **E-invoice generators (active)** | **9.92 lakh** | Jun 2025 | [GSTN GST@8 / PIB](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressNoteDetails.aspx?id=154789) |
| **E-invoice recipients (registered)** | **81.88 lakh** | Jun 2025 | (same) |
| E-way bill registered users | 55.23 lakh | Jun 2025 | (same) |
| E-way bills monthly latest | 13.84 cr (Dec 2025) | Dec 2025 | [SAG Infotech](https://blog.saginfotech.com/gst-e-way-bill-generation-hits-second-highest-ever-jan-2026) |

**Implication.** From ~7,000 cos at the ₹500 cr threshold (Oct 2020) to ~9.92 lakh active generators today (post-₹5 cr threshold) is a **100x growth** in the addressable enterprise base in 5 years, driven entirely by regulatory ratchet. This expansion has *no precedent* in any other vertical SaaS market and is the single biggest tailwind for CAOS-class platforms.

The 30-day reporting time-limit (live for AATO ≥ ₹10 cr from Apr 2025) tightens the operational pressure further — every B2B transaction must hit IRP within 30 days or the IRN cannot be generated retrospectively. That is a hard deadline that drives demand for in-flight invoice intelligence (the [P1 + P2 primitives](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/)).

The next ratchet step would be ₹3 cr or ₹2 cr; the Government has signalled this directionally but no notification yet. If it goes to ₹2 cr, the addressable B2B-invoicing population grows to roughly 25-35 lakh entities — which is closer to the 1.51 cr GST taxpayer ceiling than today's 9.92 lakh.

## Wave 3 — BRSR mandate (the ESG ratchet for listed cos)

Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting (BRSR) is SEBI's mandatory ESG disclosure for listed companies. The structure is two-tier: full BRSR (currently top 1,000) plus BRSR Core (a subset of 9 KPIs requiring third-party limited assurance, on a glide path).

### The mandate timeline

| Filing year | Top-N listed (mcap) required to file BRSR | Top-N required to file BRSR Core | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| FY 2022-23 | **Top 1,000** | — | [SEBI BRSR circular](https://www.sebi.gov.in/legal/circulars/jul-2023/brsr-core-framework-for-assurance-and-esg-disclosures-for-value-chain_73854.html) |
| FY 2023-24 | Top 1,000 | **Top 150** | (same), [DataTracks](https://datatracks.com/in/blog/top-1000-listed-companies-to-file-esg-reports-with-sebi/) |
| FY 2024-25 | Top 1,000 | **Top 250** | [Greenplaces](https://greenplaces.com/regulation/sebi-business-responsbility-sustainability-report/) |
| FY 2025-26 | Top 1,000 | **Top 500** | (same) |
| FY 2026-27 | Top 1,000 | **Top 1,000** | [KPMG firstnotes](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmgsites/in/pdf/2025/01/firstnotes-sebi-introduces-certain-key-changes-in-brsr-reporting.pdf.coredownload.inline.pdf) |

By FY26-27 every BRSR-filing listed co must also file BRSR Core with limited assurance. That assurance market is captured almost entirely by the [Big-6 audit firms](http://www.primedatabasegroup.com/newsroom/BIG_6_CONTINUE_DOMINANCE_OF_INDIAN_AUDIT_LANDSCAPE_2024-25.pdf) and a handful of mid-tier firms.

**Implication for CAOS.** BRSR is data-collection-from-plants + evidence-locker + assurance-walkthrough — three primitives ([P4 + P5 + P10](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/)) CAOS already builds. The TAM math is in the [tam sizing page](/caos/market/tam-sizing/): top 1,000 × ₹1-3L/mo = ₹120-360 cr addressable for the BRSR module alone, plus the natural pull-through into the broader Audit Workspace (Phase 3 of the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/)).

The probability that BRSR is *rolled back* is low: SEBI's track record on ESG disclosure has been monotonic (only expansion, never contraction) for 15 years, and the BRSR Core glide path was deliberately phased to absorb industry pushback over multiple years.

## Wave 4 — MSME 45-day clock + Sec 43B(h)

This is the regulatory wedge that makes accurate vendor MSME-status tracking a *tax compliance* issue for buyers, not just a payments issue.

### What changed

| Element | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Section 43B(h) of the Income Tax Act | Inserted via Finance Act 2023; effective FY 2023-24 (AY 2024-25) | [ClearTax 43B(h)](https://cleartax.in/s/section-43bh) |
| **Mechanism** | Payments to Micro & Small enterprises (Udyam-registered) beyond the MSME-Act 45-day window are **not deductible in the year of accrual** — only in the year of actual payment | (same) |
| MSMED Act 45-day rule | Buyer must pay supplier within 45 days from acceptance/deemed-acceptance (15 days if no agreement) | [MyDigitalFiling](https://mydigitalfiling.com/blog/msme-payment-delay/) |
| Udyam-registered MSMEs (supplier-side universe) | **7.83 cr** (Feb 2026) | [PIB](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2246892) |
| MSME-1 form (half-yearly disclosure of dues > 45 days) | Mandatory for all companies; quarterly enforcement increasingly strict | [ClearTax MSME-1](https://cleartax.in/s/msme-form-1) |

**Why this matters.** Before Sec 43B(h), late payment to an MSME supplier was a *commercial* problem. After Sec 43B(h), it is a *tax deduction* problem — a ₹10 lakh invoice paid 50 days late means the buyer cannot claim ₹10 lakh as expense in that year, paying ~25-30% extra tax. For a company with 200 vendors of which 80 are Micro/Small, this is a continuous compliance burden requiring:

- Real-time tracking of which vendors are MSME-registered (and which tier)
- Watchlist of unpaid invoices approaching the 45-day mark
- MSME-1 half-yearly disclosure with accurate ageing
- Year-end disallowance computation tied to the IT return

This maps directly to earlier work's [use case #3 (MSME 45-day payment + 43B(h) provisioning)](/caos/method/data-atlas/) at WTP ₹30K-1L/mo. The verified supplier-side universe (7.83 cr Udyam MSMEs) and the buyer-side regulatory pressure (every Indian company with ≥ Micro/Small vendors) make this a market-wide, not niche, demand.

The probability of Sec 43B(h) being repealed is low — the political message of "protect small businesses from late payment" is bipartisan, and the revenue impact to government from disallowance is positive.

## Wave 5 — Sec 194Q (TDS on purchases) — and the 206C(1H) reversal

This is the most counterintuitive of the five waves because *one of the two* related sections was actually *removed* in Budget 2025 — important to get right because earlier research had assumed both stayed.

### Sec 194Q — active

| Element | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger | Buyer with turnover > ₹10 cr in preceding FY purchases goods > ₹50 lakh per seller per year | [ClearTax 194Q](https://cleartax.in/s/an-overview-on-section-194q-of-the-income-tax-act-1961-ita) |
| Rate | 0.1% of value exceeding ₹50 lakh | (same) |
| Status | **Active** — no Budget 2025 changes | (same) |

Sec 194Q creates a continuous threshold-monitoring requirement: every buyer with > ₹10 cr turnover must track purchases per seller and start deducting TDS the moment any single seller crosses ₹50 lakh in a financial year. Manual tracking breaks past 100-200 vendors; ERPs don't natively compute it; this is precisely the [P2 + P7 + P9 primitive](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/) combination CAOS ships.

### Sec 206C(1H) — REMOVED in Budget 2025

| Element | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Old mechanism (2020-2025) | TCS on sale of goods > ₹50 lakh by sellers with turnover > ₹10 cr — at 0.1% | (historical) |
| **Budget 2025 change** | **Sec 206C(1H) removed effective 1 April 2025** | [TaxGuru — TCS removed FAQ](https://taxguru.in/income-tax/tcs-sale-goods-removed-april-1-2025-faqs.html) |

This matters for CAOS product spec: the earlier research use case #8 ("Sec 194Q / 206C(1H) cross-charge tracking") needs to be amended to remove the 206C(1H) leg. The 194Q leg remains a real workflow.

The broader point is that the regulatory perimeter is *not* monotonically expanding — individual provisions can be retired (as 206C(1H) was) when the cost of compliance exceeds the revenue benefit. CAOS's product roadmap needs to track this in real time, not assume static rules.

## Why these tailwinds are independent

The five waves are caused by five different regulatory bodies (RBI/Sahamati, GSTN/CBIC, SEBI, MoMSME, CBDT) and five different policy logics (consent-driven DPI, indirect-tax automation, ESG disclosure, MSME protection, direct-tax base widening). The probability of *all five* slowing at once is very low; the probability of *any one* reversing is also low because each has been written with multi-year glide paths and strong political backing.

| Wave | Driving body | Probability of reversal | What kills it |
|---|---|---|---|
| DPI maturity (AA + DigiLocker + UDIN) | RBI / NeGD / ICAI | Very low | Major data-protection backlash; no near-term signal |
| E-invoice ratchet | GSTN / CBIC | Very low | Industry pushback against further threshold drops; partial only |
| BRSR mandate | SEBI | Very low | Direction is monotonically expanding |
| MSME 45-day + 43B(h) | MoMSME / CBDT | Low | Bipartisan support; revenue-positive for govt |
| Sec 194Q | CBDT | Low | Already simpler than 206C(1H) which was repealed; could see threshold raised but not removed |

**Implication for CAOS.** Each wave is independent demand-creation; CAOS's SKU portfolio touches all five simultaneously. Practice SKU rides DPI + UDIN + GSTAT. Business SKU rides e-invoice + 194Q + 43B(h). Enterprise SKU rides BRSR + NFRA + listed-co compliance. Even if one wave slows, the others compound.

## Cross-link to the flywheel

The five tailwinds map onto the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) phases as follows:

| Phase | Primary tailwinds it rides |
|---|---|
| Phase 1 (CA-led, Y1) | UDIN + GSTAT + ICAI advertising reform |
| Phase 2 (vendor network, Y2) | E-invoice ratchet + 194Q + MSME 45-day + 43B(h) |
| Phase 3 (audit workspace, Y3) | BRSR + BRSR Core + NFRA expansion |
| Phase 4 (DPI fact graph, Y4-5) | AA at scale + DPDP Act + DigiLocker |

This is the load-bearing reason the composite flywheel is sequenced this way — each phase ignites when the relevant tailwinds are at maturity, not before. Trying to ship Phase 4 in Y1 (when AA was at 5M monthly consents) would have failed; shipping Phase 4 in Y4 (when AA is plausibly at 60-80M monthly consents) is exactly when the institutional consumer side is ready to pay for graph access.
