The CAOS earlier research sized the addressable market at **₹141-364 cr** by counting CA firms × per-seat ARPU. That is the bottom of one segment of one buyer at one ARPU band. This page rebuilds the TAM from verified anchors — third-party industry research at the top, regulator-published entity counts in the middle, named-comparable revenue figures at the bottom — and arrives at an addressable platform TAM that is **10-30x larger** in the multi-thousand crore range.

Every ARPU band in this page is anchored to either a published competitor benchmark or an industry-standard comparable. Where bands are wide, the spread is called out explicitly so investors and operators can apply their own probability weighting.

## The top-down anchor: IMARC India accounting software

The single most-cited third-party anchor for the Indian accounting/compliance software market.

| Metric | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **India accounting software market (2024)** | **US$640M** (~₹5,400 cr) | [IMARC Group](https://www.imarcgroup.com/india-accounting-software-market) |
| **Forecast (2033)** | **US$1,417M** (~₹12,000 cr) | [IMARC Group](https://www.imarcgroup.com/india-accounting-software-market) |
| Implied CAGR | **9.2%** | derived |

Two notes on this anchor. First, IMARC's category covers accounting + compliance software collectively — it is **not** a CA-only or PMS-only category. Second, this is the *current* and *forecast* spend, not the *addressable* spend; addressable TAM is typically 2-4x stated category spend in immature markets where penetration is low.

For India in 2024, software penetration in compliance is by every available indicator under 20% (most mid-market companies still run Tally + Excel + email + WhatsApp). That implies the *true* addressable spend if penetration normalised to global benchmarks is in the range of **₹15,000-30,000 cr** today, growing to **₹35,000-60,000 cr** by 2033.

The CAOS thesis is to capture a meaningful share of that uplift via the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) — not to displace IMARC's installed base.

## The bottom-up: layer-by-layer addressable

The four-SKU stack maps onto the population layers from the [market overview](/caos/market/overview/). Each row below is computed bottom-up: entities × adoption probability × ARPU band.

### Layer 1 — Practice SKU (the practitioner wedge)

| Input | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| ICAI registered firms | **1,00,138** | [The Finance Story Oct 2025](https://thefinancestory.com/mid-sized-indian-ca-firms-employing-20-percent-audit-workforce) |
| Avg seats per firm | 5 | derived from firm-size distribution: 72.6% sole-prop (1.5 staff avg) + 21.7% 2-3 partner (2.4 staff avg) + 3.6% 4-5 partner (4.7 staff avg) + 2.1% 6+ partner (19+ staff avg) per [The Finance Story](https://thefinancestory.com/mid-sized-indian-ca-firms-employing-20-percent-audit-workforce) |
| ARPU per seat per month | ₹500-1,500 | benchmarked against [Zoho Practice (₹2,950/firm/mo)](/caos/competitors/zoho-practice/) and existing CA-PMS pricing in the [comparison matrix](/caos/competitors/comparison-matrix/) |
| Adjacent practitioners (CS + CMA + IP + Valuer) | ~1.85 lakh combined | [overview page](/caos/market/overview/) |
| Per-entity surcharge per month (50-100 client entities × ₹100-500/entity) | ₹5K-50K/firm/mo | derived from competitor positioning (PMS + entity-based add-ons) |

**Practice SKU addressable TAM:**
- Seat-based: 1,00,138 firms × 5 seats × ₹1,000/seat/mo × 12 = **₹600 cr** (midpoint, CA only)
- Per-entity-based: 1,00,138 firms × 75 entities (avg) × ₹250/entity/mo × 12 = **₹2,250 cr** (high end if pricing model includes per-entity)
- Adjacent practitioners (CSs + CMAs + IPs + valuers — narrower segments at lower per-seat ARPU): **~₹150-300 cr** combined
- **Total Practice SKU addressable: ~₹500-2,500 cr** depending on pricing model

This range is 5-15x larger than the ₹141-364 cr CA-PMS estimate — primarily because earlier sizing used only seat-based pricing for the CA-firm segment alone, and did not count per-entity surcharge or adjacent practitioner bodies.

**Business SKU addressable TAM:**
- Lower-mid (~5,000 cos × ₹1L/mo × 12) = **₹600 cr**
- Upper-mid (~3,000 cos × ₹2L/mo × 12) = **₹720 cr**
- Lower-large (~2,000 cos × ₹8L/mo × 12) = **₹1,920 cr**
- Sub-total mid-market: **~₹3,200 cr** at midpoint

The spread (₹3,000-15,000 cr) is the single most consequential range in this entire docs corpus. The low end assumes CAOS reaches 5-10% of the sweet spot only; the high end assumes 15-25% penetration of the broader GST universe. The probability-weighted midpoint is roughly **₹5,000-7,000 cr** addressable — already an order of magnitude larger than the CA-PMS-only TAM.

**Enterprise SKU addressable TAM:**
- Listed cos (in-house Audit Workspace + BRSR + multi-entity consolidation): 8,803 × 30% adoption × ₹6L/mo midpoint × 12 = **~₹1,900 cr** at midpoint adoption
- Listed cos at high penetration: 8,803 × 50% × ₹9L midpoint × 12 = **~₹4,800 cr**
- Audit-firm side (Big-6 + mid-tier + ~5K firm-engagements): ~5,000 engagements × ₹50K = **₹25 cr**
- BRSR-only (top 1,000 × ₹1-3L/mo specifically for BRSR Core module): **₹120-360 cr**
- **Total Enterprise SKU addressable: ~₹2,000-5,000 cr** depending on adoption depth

The Enterprise SKU per-deal ARPU is 10-100x the Practice SKU. A single Tata / Reliance / Bharti enterprise contract at ₹15L/mo = the same ARR as ~250 CA firms at ₹500/seat × 5 seats. The flywheel logic is: use Phase 1 (CA-led) to build trust, Phase 2 (vendor network) to build data, Phase 3 (audit workspace) to land enterprise — at which point the same 250-CA-equivalent ARR comes in single contracts.

## Putting it together

| SKU / Layer | Low estimate | Midpoint | High estimate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Practice SKU** | ₹500 cr | ₹1,500 cr | ₹2,500 cr |
| **Business SKU** | ₹3,000 cr | ₹6,000 cr | ₹15,000 cr |
| **Enterprise SKU** | ₹2,000 cr | ₹3,500 cr | ₹5,000 cr |
| **Adjacent segments** | ₹900 cr | ₹2,000 cr | ₹4,500 cr |
| **Total addressable platform TAM** | **₹6,400 cr** | **₹13,000 cr** | **₹27,000 cr** |

The midpoint **₹13,000 cr** is roughly 2.4x the IMARC India accounting software TAM (₹5,400 cr 2024). That gap is *expected*: IMARC sizes the *category as it is*, not the addressable spend that would exist at global-benchmark penetration. Compliance + financial-document-fabric SaaS is structurally underbuilt in India relative to similar emerging markets, and the regulatory ratchet (e-invoice, BRSR, MSME 45-day, Sec 194Q — covered on the [tailwinds page](/caos/market/regulatory-tailwinds/)) is closing that gap rapidly.

## Why file 01's ₹141-364 cr TAM was understated

The original earlier research sizing went like this:

> CA-PMS adoption is &lt;2% of 1.59L COP holders. At 5K COP holders ÷ 5 staff = 1,000 firms paying ~₹15K/yr = ₹1.5 cr revenue today. Addressable: 1.59L COP × ₹15-30K/yr ARPU = ₹150-300 cr.

Three problems with that math:

1. **Single SKU, single buyer**. earlier sizing used only Practice SKU at the seat-pricing band. It did not count per-entity surcharge, did not count adjacent practitioners (CS, CMA, IP, valuer), did not count Business SKU (in-house finance teams), did not count Enterprise SKU (listed cos), did not count adjacent segments (vCFO firms, lender data subscriptions).

2. **The ARPU band was too narrow**. earlier sizing used ₹15-30K/yr per firm — anchored only against current CA-PMS pricing. The Business SKU at ₹50K-3L/mo and Enterprise SKU at ₹3-15L/mo are 10-100x higher per buyer.

3. **It treated TAM as static**. The regulatory ratchet (e-invoice ₹500 cr → ₹5 cr, BRSR top 150 → 1,000, MSME 45-day) is *expanding* the addressable pool every year — covered on the [regulatory tailwinds page](/caos/market/regulatory-tailwinds/). Static TAM math captures none of that growth.

The corrected platform TAM at midpoint **₹13,000 cr** is roughly **40-90x** the CA-PMS-only estimate — and even that is conservative because it assumes today's regulatory perimeter, not the 2027-2030 perimeter when DPDP Act + BRSR Core (top 1,000) + AA at 60-80M monthly consents + ICAI's 30 lakh CA target are all in motion.

## Where this TAM runs out

The TAM math says addressable spend is ₹6,400-27,000 cr at midpoint ₹13,000 cr. The flywheel math ([sizing math →](/caos/flywheel/sizing-math/)) says the *serviceable obtainable* market for CAOS in 60 months is ₹150-500 cr ARR at the Y5 stage — a 1-4% share of the addressable platform TAM.

That 1-4% share assumption is conservative; ClearTax has been operating ~10 years to reach the comparable scale, and CAOS's 60-month plan is more aggressive on Phase-3 enterprise capture than ClearTax's enterprise tier. Whether 1-4% becomes 5-10% depends on three independent variables:

- **Phase-2 vendor-network ignition** — does the ~17 lakh validated vendor count from Phase 2 produce 1-5% conversion to paid Business SKU?
- **Phase-3 audit-workspace adoption rate** — do 5 of 6 Big-6 firms standardise on CAOS as the auditor-client bridge by Year 4?
- **Phase-4 DPI lender-side monetisation** — does CAOS capture even 1% of AA-mediated loans (₹1.67 lakh cr in FY25 alone) as referral fees?

If 2 of 3 hit, CAOS reaches the high end of the obtainable range. If 1 of 3 hits, it stays in the mid range. The decision points are detailed in [Decisions D1-D7](/caos/decisions/d1-expand-to-finance-teams/).

:::tip[The sizing one-liner]
**Addressable platform TAM is ₹6,400-27,000 cr (midpoint ₹13,000 cr). Serviceable obtainable in 60 months is ₹150-500 cr ARR (1-4% share). The cap is flywheel ignition velocity, not market size.**
:::
