Each loop activates only when the previous has reached its ignition threshold. Each one *reuses the data and trust accumulated by the previous*. The composite is more defensible than any single loop because multiple network effects compound in different directions. See [the composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) for the operational form, and [the four candidates](/caos/flywheel/four-candidates/) for the alternatives that were rejected.

## Why each loop compounds

- **Phase 1 — CA-Led Practitioner Network** ([details](/caos/flywheel/phase-1-ca-led/)). Compounds because 28K+ Tally partners ([source](https://tallysolutions.com/about-tally/)), 1.59 lakh COP-holding CAs ([source](https://finance.careers360.com/articles/icai-student-and-member-report-2025)), and 1.7 cr UDINs/year already run on shared digital rails. Each CA-onboarded entity becomes a PAN/GSTIN-tagged node for every later loop.
- **Phase 2 — Vendor-Network Fact Graph** ([details](/caos/flywheel/phase-2-vendor-network/)). Compounds because 9.92 lakh e-invoice generators reach 81.88 lakh registered recipients ([source](https://a2ztaxcorp.net/gst-8-years-report-released-gstn-highlights-record-revenue-compliance-milestones/)); Sec 194Q + MSME 45-day clock + Sec 43B(h) legally compel accurate vendor masters; ~30% statistical vendor overlap cuts onboarding cost for each new entrant.
- **Phase 3 — Audit-Workspace Bridge** ([details](/caos/flywheel/phase-3-audit-workspace/)). Compounds because Big-6 audit 67% of Nifty 500 (326 of 483) ([source](http://www.primedatabasegroup.com/newsroom/BIG_6_CONTINUE_DOMINANCE_OF_INDIAN_AUDIT_LANDSCAPE_2024-25.pdf)); NFRA's 94 disciplinary orders ([source](https://taxguru.in/company-law/action-nfra-prevent-audit-failure-regulatory-lapses.html)) push structured tooling; the 20-audits-per-partner statutory cap concentrates the buyer.
- **Phase 4 — DPI Fact Graph** ([details](/caos/flywheel/phase-4-dpi-fact-graph/)). Compounds because AA is at 22.31M monthly consents (Mar 2026), +78.6% YoY ([source](https://sahamati.org.in/aa-dashboard/)); 1,157 ecosystem participants ready as institutional consumers; ₹1.67 lakh cr lending facilitated via AA in FY25 alone ([source](https://sahamati.org.in/the-credit-reimagined-account-aggregator-aa-impact-report-h2-fy25/)); DPDP Act enforcement (May 2027) makes consent-driven sharing the legal default.

## Why composite, not single

No single flywheel is right at every horizon. The four-candidate analysis ([details](/caos/flywheel/four-candidates/)) makes the trade-offs explicit:

- **CA-Led** — lowest activation energy, lowest ceiling. Uses pre-built ICAI / Tally / Master-partner rails. But 72% of CAs are time-poor sole practitioners; portal-user to paid conversion unproven at India scale.
- **Vendor-Network** — strongest compounding, cannot ignite cold. Plaid-style: vendor verified once is pre-onboarded for every enterprise that pays them. Needs critical mass of enterprises first; needs DPDP-compliant consent design.
- **Audit-Workspace** — sharpest enterprise wedge, slowest sales motion. Once Big-6 standardizes, replacement risk is near-zero. But 12-18 month sales cycles; SOC 2 + ISO 27001 + DPDP pre-conditions; mid-tier (BDO, Grant Thornton) is realistic Y2, Big-4 deferred to Y3.
- **DPI-Graph** — highest ceiling, years of data accumulation needed. Network + regulatory + integration depth combined; practically impossible to replicate at scale once established. But 3-5 year payoff; AA TSP / FIU certification + GSP partner contracts + DPDP-compliant consent flows are heavy build.

Use each loop where it is strongest; let the next loop ride on the trust and data the previous one built. CA-Led leaves PAN/GSTIN-tagged nodes that Vendor-Network invites cheaply. Vendor-Network builds the validated counterparty graph that Audit Workspace references. Audit Workspace lands the listed-co adoptions whose AA-flowing data makes DPI Graph institutionally credible. Each loop's failure mode is mitigated by the loop after it.

## What you cannot see in the picture

Three things compound silently. **Mapping memory** (P9 of the [atomic primitives](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/)) — every counterparty-to-ledger rule, every BRS auto-match learned once is reusable across firm scope and (when promoted) network scope; makes Year-2 onboarding 30-50% cheaper than Year-1. **Compliance-grade audit trail** (P11) — immutable, append-only, replayable; makes ICAI peer review, NFRA inspection, IFC controls testing, and SOC 2 audits possible without re-engineering. **The fact graph** — GST + IT + ROC + bank via AA + invoices, all PAN/GSTIN-keyed; sold to CAs today, available to lenders, auditors, investors, and M&A diligence tomorrow. The original research captured only the CA use of this graph.
