CAOS is a platform thesis for the messy edges of Indian compliance — the paperwork, deadlines, reconciliations, notices, audits, and counterparty workflows that ERPs do not own and Excel cannot scale. It works above accounting systems (Tally, SAP, Oracle, Zoho) rather than replacing them, and it serves the same atomic primitives to a Chartered Accountant practising in Pune, a CFO at a ₹400 cr manufacturing company in Chennai, and a Big-6 audit team running a Nifty-500 statutory audit in Mumbai.

The platform sits on three load-bearing observations. First, the buyer is unusually homogeneous: **60.8% of all ICAI Chartered Accountants work in industry** as CFOs, controllers, internal auditors and finance heads, which means the in-house finance buyer is statistically often herself a CA — credibility transfer between practitioner and corporate buyer is structurally free. Second, the same 15 [atomic primitives](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/) — document capture, classification, extraction, mapping memory, recurring-deadline calendar, counterparty master, evidence locker, notice lifecycle, reconciliation engine, maker-checker workflow — power both a CA's practice and a corporate finance team's close. Third, India's Digital Public Infrastructure (Account Aggregator, GSP, ERI, DigiLocker, eMudhra DSC) is at near-enterprise scale, with **22.31 million AA consents flowing in March 2026 alone, growing 78.6% year-on-year**, and is the substrate on which the eventual fact graph compounds.

The output is a [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) with four sequenced loops over 60 months — CA-Led practitioner network in Year 1, Vendor-Network fact graph in Year 2, Audit Workspace bridge in Year 3, DPI-powered fact graph in Years 4–5 — each reusing the data and trust accumulated by the previous.

## Market at a glance

<CardGrid stagger>
	<Card title="4,07,629 ICAI members" icon="rocket">
		Across 1,00,138 registered firms (72.6% sole-prop). **60.8% (2,48,072) work in industry** as CFOs, controllers, finance heads — the in-house buyer is, statistically, often herself a Chartered Accountant. ([source](https://finance.careers360.com/articles/icai-student-and-member-report-2025))
	</Card>
	<Card title="18.17 lakh active companies" icon="document">
		Of 28.05 lakh MCA-registered. Plus **1.51 cr active GST taxpayers** and **7.83 cr Udyam-registered MSMEs** (Feb 2026). The buyer base is two orders of magnitude wider than the practitioner base. ([MCA](https://www.business-standard.com/companies/news/over-2-8-million-companies-registered-in-india-65-active-govt-data-125021800695_1.html), [GSTN](https://a2ztaxcorp.net/gst-8-years-report-released-gstn-highlights-record-revenue-compliance-milestones/), [PIB Udyam](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2246892))
	</Card>
	<Card title="22.31 million AA consents/month" icon="puzzle">
		India's Account Aggregator framework handled **22.31 million consents in March 2026** (+78.6% YoY) across 1,157 ecosystem participants, facilitating ₹1.67 lakh cr of lending in FY25 alone. The substrate for consent-driven finance is operational at scale. ([Sahamati dashboard](https://sahamati.org.in/aa-dashboard/))
	</Card>
	<Card title="100x e-invoice expansion in 5 years" icon="list-format">
		The e-invoice mandate ratcheted from a ₹500 cr turnover threshold (Oct 2020) to ₹5 cr (Aug 2023). The addressable enterprise base for compliance software grew from a few thousand to **9.92 lakh active e-invoice generators** with **81.88 lakh registered recipients**. ([GSTN](https://a2ztaxcorp.net/gst-8-years-report-released-gstn-highlights-record-revenue-compliance-milestones/), [ClearTax](https://cleartax.in/s/e-invoicing-businesses-above-rs-5-crore-turnover))
	</Card>
	<Card title="28K+ Tally partners" icon="setting">
		140 Master + 18,000+ Secondary partners; Tally itself reports 2.5–2.7 million customers, ₹710 cr revenue FY25. The "layer above Tally" wedge has pre-built distribution rails. ([source](https://tallysolutions.com/about-tally/), [Inc42](https://inc42.com/features/how-tally-strikes-a-balance-between-enabling-ai-adoption-and-growth/))
	</Card>
	<Card title="Big-6 own 67% of Nifty 500 audits" icon="approve-check">
		EY + Deloitte + PwC + KPMG + Grant Thornton + BDO audit 326 of 483 Nifty 500 companies. The Audit Workspace bridge sells to a concentrated buyer: ~15 partnership conversations cover 80% of the listed corporate buyer pool. ([PrimeDatabase](http://www.primedatabasegroup.com/newsroom/BIG_6_CONTINUE_DOMINANCE_OF_INDIAN_AUDIT_LANDSCAPE_2024-25.pdf))
	</Card>
