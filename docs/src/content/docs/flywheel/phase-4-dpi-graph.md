Phase 4 is where CAOS becomes the Plaid + Carta + ERP-snapshot of Indian businesses — consent-mediated, AA-flowing, lender-priced. By Year 4-5, 5-10 lakh entities have active AA / GSP / ERI / MCA flows into CAOS, and 50+ lender partners pay subscription access for verified entity data. The ARR ceiling for this phase alone is ₹150-500 cr. The verified data infrastructure is already at near-enterprise scale.

## The loop in 5 steps

### Step 1 — Each entity connects its data via DPI rails

Each entity already on CAOS (from [Phase 1](/caos/flywheel/phase-1-ca-led/), [Phase 2](/caos/flywheel/phase-2-vendor-network/), or [Phase 3](/caos/flywheel/phase-3-audit-workspace/)) connects:

- **Bank statements via AA** (consent-driven, [22.31M monthly consents Mar 2026](https://sahamati.org.in/aa-dashboard/) means the underlying rail is at scale)
- **GST data via GSP** ([62 empanelled GSPs](https://www.microvistatech.com/blog/gst-suvidha-providers-gsp-list-2025/) available as integration partners)
- **Income-tax data via ERI** ([50+ named ERIs](https://cleartax.in/s/e-return-intermediary-eri-income-tax) available; CAOS can become ERI Type 2 in Year 3 per the verified-numbers atlas D7)
- **MCA21 data via aggregator** (80.26 lakh MCA21 forms filed in 10 months FY24-25 — the underlying volume is real)
- **EPFO + ESIC data via portal connectors** ([21.04 lakh EPFO net members added in July 2025 alone](https://www.epfindia.gov.in/site_docs/PDFs/EPFO_PRESS_RELEASES/EPFO_RecordsAdditionOf21.04%20LakhNetMembersDuring%20uly2025.pdf))
- **DigiLocker for incorporation / KYC documents** ([62 cr+ users; 950 cr+ documents](https://www.digilocker.gov.in/))

The consent infrastructure is already in place at the regulator level. CAOS adds the workflow layer: a single consent token + a single dashboard surfaces compliance-and-cash-and-counterparty health for each entity.

### Step 2 — The entity's compliance + cash + graph health is live and indexed

Each entity now has a real-time, PAN/GSTIN/CIN-anchored entity profile:

- **Compliance health** — GST returns filing status, ITR filing status, MCA21 filing status, statutory dues paid, audit completion status
- **Cash flow** — last 12 months bank statement (consent-flowing via AA), monthly inflow/outflow trends, peak-day balances, cheque-bounce indicators
- **Counterparty graph** — top vendors with verified GSTIN, top customers with payment history, MSME-tagged exposures, related-party transactions
- **Employee growth** — EPFO new joiners per month, ESIC payroll trends, attrition rates
- **Statutory deduction history** — TDS filed vs deposited, GST RCM compliance, TCS history (post-Sec 206C(1H) removal)

The single entity profile is what makes CAOS an *underwriting-ready data source*, not just a SaaS tool.

### Step 3 — Lenders, investors, auditors, advisors subscribe to graph access

The institutional consumer side wants this data:

- **Lenders** ([1,157 AA participants](https://sahamati.org.in/fip-fiu-in-account-aggregators-ecosystem/)) — for working-capital, term-loan, supply-chain-finance underwriting
- **NBFCs** (~12,000 NBFCs registered with RBI) — same use case, often with thinner credit teams that benefit more from pre-cleaned data
- **Investors** (PE / VC / IPO underwriters) — for due diligence, especially on SME-IPO targets
- **Audit firms** (Big-6 + mid-tier) — for client-acceptance evaluation and ongoing risk assessment
- **Insurance underwriters** (general + life + credit insurers) — for SME risk pricing
- **Trade-credit insurers** (Coface, Atradius India, etc.) — for buyer / supplier creditworthiness

CAOS becomes the Plaid + Carta + ERP-snapshot of Indian businesses — consent-mediated under the [DPDP Act enforcement window (May 2027)](https://www.meity.gov.in/).

### Step 4 — Free entity tier funded by institutional subscriptions

With institutional consumer subscriptions paying for graph access, CAOS can offer **free basic features to enterprises** in exchange for graph access (with explicit consent at each share). This flips the funnel: entity adoption is no longer gated by SaaS pricing. The entity sees value because their data shareability becomes a credit *asset*.

This is the same model that made Plaid the default in US fintech: end-users (consumers) get free bank-data connectivity; institutional consumers (fintech apps) pay for graph access. CAOS applies the same architecture to Indian businesses + Indian institutional capital.

### Step 5 — Cross-loop: AA-mediated lending creates SME demand for CAOS

The fifth step closes the loop into the lending market. [₹1.67 lakh cr of loans were facilitated via AA in FY25 alone](https://sahamati.org.in/the-credit-reimagined-account-aggregator-aa-impact-report-h2-fy25/), with [1.89 cr loan accounts disbursed through AA-mediated underwriting](https://sahamati.org.in/the-credit-reimagined-account-aggregator-aa-impact-report-h2-fy25/). CAOS-onboarded SMEs see *better* loan terms because their data is consent-shareable, audit-confirmed (Phase 3), and counterparty-validated (Phase 2).

Adoption is now **pulled by SME demand for credit**, not just compliance. This is the highest-velocity acquisition mechanism the platform will ever have.

## The magic moment

> **SME entity on CAOS gets a credit offer from a partner lender at 0.5% lower interest than market** — because their CAOS-mediated data is underwriting-ready.
>
> The mechanism: the SME has been on CAOS for 6+ months. Their GST returns are filed (verified via GSP), their ITR is filed (verified via ERI), their MCA filings are current (verified via MCA21 aggregator), their statutory dues are paid (verified via TDS / EPFO / ESIC connectors), their last 12 months of bank statements are AA-shareable (with consent), their top vendors are verified counterparties ([Phase 2 graph](/caos/flywheel/phase-2-vendor-network/)), and their statutory audit (if applicable) is documented in the [Phase 3 workspace](/caos/flywheel/phase-3-audit-workspace/).
>
> When the SME applies for a working-capital line, the partner lender receives all of this as a single, consented, structured underwriting bundle. The lender's own credit team spends 30 minutes instead of 10 days on the file. The lender prices the loan 0.5% below market because their own cost-of-underwriting is structurally lower.
>
> The SME pays less interest. The lender writes more loans with less risk. CAOS earns a referral fee (1-3% of loan value).
>
> **Three-sided value creation.** This is what calls the verified-data-anchored-DPI-flywheel — and it is the closing loop of the 60-month composite.

The product surface required:

- A consent dashboard
- An AA TSP / FIU integration (verified [17 NBFC-AAs operational](https://sahamati.org.in/account-aggregators-in-india/))
- A lender-side API with consent-mediated entity data delivery
- A referral-fee tracking + reconciliation engine
- An entity-side "credit health" score that the SME can see and improve

## Failure modes for Phase 4

Three failure scenarios specific to Phase 4:

| Failure | Trigger | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **DPI integration costs exceed projections** | AA TSP licensing + GSP partner contracts + ERI Type 2 status involve regulatory cost + compliance overhead | Partner with existing AA TSPs (CAMSFinServ, Anumati, Perfios) and existing GSPs (Cygnet, IRIS) rather than build in-house |
| **AA framework collapses or stalls** | Probability low ([78.6% YoY growth](https://www.business-standard.com/finance/news/aa-ecosystem-consent-processing-up-78-in-fy25-shows-sahamati-data-125031301053_1.html), govt-backed) | Phase 1-3 still work without DPI; only Phase 4 ceiling is reduced |
| **DPDP enforcement is so aggressive that consent friction breaks SME workflows** | Probability medium (post May 2027) | Consent UX is CAOS's competitive frontier; design for compliance from Day 1 |
