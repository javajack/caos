This page is the honest list of what the research **does not yet know** despite the verified-numbers atlas + 4-agent research pass that produced [the verified-numbers atlas](/caos/method/data-atlas/). Twelve priority items need primary research (RTIs to government bodies, commissioned surveys, vendor interviews) before being safely cited in pitch decks or product specs. Twenty-two further investigations from (/caos/method/data-atlas/) round out the queue.

The queue exists for two audiences:

1. **Internal** — the next research budget allocation. Estimated ₹3-5 lakh + 30-40 person-days to clear the critical tier.
2. **External transparency** — readers who want to know what's solidly grounded vs what's still speculative. Linked from [the data atlas](/caos/method/data-atlas/) and [Research approach](/caos/method/data-atlas/).

## Estimated next-round budget

<Aside type="tip" title="The ask">
**Critical tier (G1-G12 + R1-R6)**: ~₹3-5 lakh + 90-120 person-days. Can be done in 8-10 calendar weeks with 2 researchers + ad-hoc engineering support.

**Output**: a tightened version of the [data atlas](/caos/method/data-atlas/) with 90%+ of unverified claims primary-source-validated; a feature-prioritised a future product spec; a Big-4 partnership pilot offer; a pricing model validated with 30+ controllers.

**Justification**: every ₹1 of next-round research budget removes ~₹1-2 lakh of risk from the Phase 0-3 build. Verifying G1 alone (the "&lt;2% PMS adoption" claim) determines whether the file-01 TAM was understated by 10x or by 30x — a ₹3,000 cr difference in board-pack defensibility.
</Aside>

## Outputs to produce alongside research

The next round should also generate three durable artifacts:

- A **fact-graph spreadsheet** — every claim in files 01-14 mapped to evidence quality (strong / medium / weak / anecdotal). Quarterly refresh.
- A **persona map poster** — 1-pager per persona with day-in-life, pain, today's hack, our wedge, WTP. Useful for sales enablement.
- A **bridge journey map** — shows the doc/notice/audit-query/JV crossing the CA-Finance team boundary; highlights where today's friction is. Useful for [Audit Workspace](/caos/flywheel/phase-3-audit-workspace/) feature spec.

## What changes when each gap is closed

A research gap closing changes a specific decision or claim:

- **G1 closes** → [TAM sizing](/caos/market/tam-sizing/) loses the "&lt;2% PMS" callout entirely; addressable TAM becomes a verified range, not an estimate
- **G2-G3 close** → [Practitioners stakeholder page](/caos/stakeholders/practitioners/) gets a tighter GSTP + ERI count
- **G4 closes** → [DPI ecosystem](/caos/gtm/dpi-ecosystem/) gets concrete partnership target counts per category
- **G5-G8 close** → [Workflow volumes](/caos/market/workflow-volumes/) becomes the canonical Indian compliance-volume reference
- **G9 closes** → [Tally / ERP integration](/caos/product/tally-erp-integration/) loses the "70-80% market share (cited)" caveat
- **G10 closes** → vCFO partner program goes from concept to spec
- **G11 closes** → MSME 45-day use case priority gets re-ranked in [Use cases](/caos/product/use-cases/)
- **G12 closes** → [Audit firm partnerships](/caos/gtm/audit-firm-partnerships/) gets engagement-level TAM
- **R2-R3-R5 close** → product spec for [Audit Workspace](/caos/flywheel/phase-3-audit-workspace/) gets locked
- **R10-R11-R14 close** → [GTM channel architecture](/caos/gtm/channel-architecture/) gets validated channel-mix economics
