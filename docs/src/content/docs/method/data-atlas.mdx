Every quantitative claim that appears anywhere else on this site — practitioner population, buyer entity universe, workflow volume, e-invoice ratchet history, tech-ecosystem capacity, in-house CA bridge insight — is reproduced here with **metric, value, as-of date, source URL, and confidence rating**. Each number can be re-verified in two clicks. The atlas is the ground truth that anchors the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/), the [stakeholder atlas](/caos/stakeholders/overview/), and the [TAM sizing](/caos/market/tam/).

Confidence ratings (High / Med / Low / Unverified) follow the [Source quality legend](/caos/method/source-quality-legend/). All currency is ₹ in Indian notation (lakh / crore). All "as of" dates reflect the latest verifiable disclosure available during the verification pass (Apr-May 2026).

One long page with anchor links is the form most useful for an investor or partner audit. Use Ctrl-F to search; use the section list immediately below to navigate.

## §4. E-invoice threshold history

The e-invoice mandate is the single fastest example of regulatory expansion narrowing the addressable buyer base — from ~700 cos at launch to ~9.92 lakh in 5 years. Each ratchet pulled millions of mid-market buyers into the addressable pool for compliance / AP / IRP-bridge software. **The addressable enterprise base for compliance software has grown ~100x in 5 years**, driven entirely by regulatory ratchet — a tailwind with no precedent in any other Indian software market.

The [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) Phase 2 (Vendor-Network) is designed to capture exactly this expansion.

## §6. The "in-house CA" insight

This is the single most strategically loaded number-finding of the entire verification pass. It reframes the buyer in a way no other number on this page does — and it is the foundation of the [single-brand decision](/caos/business-model/three-skus/), the [CA-grade positioning](/caos/strategy/usp-wedge/), and the [CA-led Phase 1 of the composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/phase-1-ca-led/).

| Metric | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| ICAI total members (Mar 2025) | **4,07,629** | [Careers360 / ICAI 2025 Report](https://finance.careers360.com/articles/icai-student-and-member-report-2025) |
| ICAI members holding Certificate of Practice | **1,59,557 (39.2%)** | [Careers360 / ICAI 2025 Report](https://finance.careers360.com/articles/icai-student-and-member-report-2025) |
| ICAI members **WITHOUT COP** (working in industry / academia / govt) | **2,48,072 (60.8%)** | [Careers360 / ICAI 2025 Report](https://finance.careers360.com/articles/icai-student-and-member-report-2025) |

These 2.48 lakh CAs are the *literal physical bridge* between the practitioner side (the practising-CA persona) and the in-house finance side (the in-house CFO / Controller personas F1, F2). They sit *inside* enterprises as CFOs, controllers, finance heads, internal auditors, tax leads. They:

- Speak the same vocabulary as the external CA — their own classmates and seniors.
- Already trust ICAI-grade content and tools — they grew up on it.
- Are the budget approvers for enterprise compliance software.
- Are the most likely first finance-team users to evaluate CAOS when their external CA recommends it.

**Implication for the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/):** the most expensive part of B2B SaaS — credibility transfer between buyer and seller — is structurally *free* in this market. The seller (CAOS with CA-side traction) speaks directly to a buyer (in-house CA) who shares regulatory upbringing. This compresses the typical 9-12 month enterprise sales cycle and makes the CA-led referral channel structurally more efficient than equivalent foreign markets.

<Aside type="tip" title="Why this changes the brand decision">
an earlier framing debated whether CAOS needed a separate brand for the in-house finance buyer ("CAOS for CAs" + "FinanceBuddy for enterprises"). The 60.8% statistic kills the dual-brand argument: the enterprise CFO buyer is, statistically, often herself a CA. **"CA-grade" is genetic to the buyer, not a positioning claim.** See [Single-brand, layered SKUs](/caos/business-model/three-skus/) for the resulting business decision.
</Aside>
