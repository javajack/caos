Every quantitative table on this site carries a **Confidence** column. That column uses one of four ratings — High, Med, Low, Unverified — derived from a deliberate, mechanical hierarchy of source authority. This page defines that hierarchy and gives examples of each rating from real tables on this site, so a reader can audit any number's pedigree in one click.

The rating is not a judgement of *importance* — a Low-confidence number can still be load-bearing for the strategy. The rating is a judgement of *citability*: it tells the reader how confident an investor, partner, or auditor can be when re-quoting the number externally.

<Aside type="caution" title="The rating travels with the cell, not the table">
Most tables on this site mix High, Med, and Low confidence rows. Tally's 2.5M customers (Med) sits next to its 28K partner network (Med) and its claimed 70-80% market share (Low) — all in the same vendor row. Read the per-row rating, not the table-level one.
</Aside>

## The 4-tier hierarchy

```
High Direct govt / regulator / professional body publication
 OR named press citing such with explicit URL
 ↓
Med Third-party aggregator citing primary source
 (Tracxn, Inc42, The Finance Story, CAClubIndia, IBEF)
 ↓
Low Vendor self-disclosure
 OR single uncorroborated press source
 ↓
Unverified Claim exists in CAOS original research or industry chatter
 but no authoritative source found
```

## Tier definitions in detail

### High — direct primary source

A High-confidence number meets *one* of these tests:

1. **Direct publication** by the government body, regulator, or professional body that owns the data — for example, PIB press releases, GSTN statistics dashboards, CBDT annual reports, MCA monthly information bulletins, ICAI Student & Member Reports, RBI bulletins, SEBI circulars, IBBI service-provider pages, NFRA disciplinary orders, EPFO press releases, Sahamati AA dashboards, DigiLocker home page metrics.
2. **Named press citing such**, with the explicit URL to the primary disclosure preserved so a reader can follow the chain — for example, Business Standard quoting MCA's monthly bulletin, The Finance Story citing the ICAI Annual Report, PIB summarising a Budget announcement.

**When to use:** Any cell where the number is load-bearing for sizing, TAM, or flywheel math. All Phase-by-phase targets in the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) anchor on High-confidence numbers.

**When to caveat:** Almost never. The only caveats applicable to High-confidence numbers are *as-of dates* (a March 2025 ICAI count is the truth as of March 2025, not today) and *definitional drift* (e.g. "Active GST taxpayers" includes regular + composition + TDS-deductors, which the source spells out).

### Med — credible aggregator citing primary

A Med-confidence number sits one level down from the primary disclosure. It comes from a third-party aggregator that *names* its source but does not host the original. The aggregator's editorial standards are credible — Tracxn, Inc42, The Finance Story, CAClubIndia, IBEF, Equitymaster — but the citation chain has one extra hop.

**When to use:** Sizing inputs where the primary source is paywalled, undisclosed, or inconvenient — for example, private-company revenue (Tally, Karbon, Vyapar), competitor funding rounds (Tracxn), or industry growth statistics (IMARC, IBEF).

**When to caveat:** State the aggregator and the as-of date. Investor materials should distinguish "Tracxn cumulative funding" (Med) from "company-disclosed ARR" (Low without an audited disclosure).

### Low — vendor self-disclosure or single weak press

A Low-confidence number rests on *one* of these footings:

1. **Vendor self-disclosure** — the company's own marketing copy, press release, or interview claim, with no third-party verification — for example, Vyapar's "1 cr customers" claim, Karbon's "3,000+ businesses" claim, vCFO firms' headcount claims.
2. **A single press source** with no corroborating publication — a one-off Business Standard quote or a single Inc42 interview.

**When to use:** When the number is genuinely useful for context but cannot be elevated. Most competitor funding figures, vCFO firm sizes, and product-claim metrics live here.

**When to caveat:** Always. Investor and partner materials should mark Low-confidence claims explicitly as "vendor-claimed" or "single-source", and treat them as upper bounds. The [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) sizing math intentionally uses High/Med anchors on the *demand* side and treats Low-confidence supply-side numbers as ceilings.

### Unverified — claim exists, no authoritative source

An Unverified claim is one that appeared in earlier research or in industry chatter but for which **no authoritative source could be located** in the verification pass — even at the Low tier.

**When to use:** Never as a load-bearing number. Only as an explicit "we cannot verify this" entry in [What we removed (and why)](/caos/method/data-atlas/). The claim is preserved for transparency; the number is not used downstream.

**When to caveat:** Always with the framing "carried in industry but not verified to a primary source"

**Examples on this site:**

| Claim | Where it appeared | Verification status |
|---|---|---|
| &lt;2% PMS adoption among CA firms | earlier research, 04 (load-bearing) | No ICAI tech-adoption survey exists; numerator (vendor-claimed Jamku 1,700 / QwikCA 20K) is mush; denominator (~1L firms) is correct. this site says "low adoption" qualitatively. |
| 30-40% of operational time on follow-ups | earlier research, 04 | No source; anecdotal. this site says "significant" qualitatively. |
| WhatsApp 3-5x faster than email | earlier research | No source; asserted. this site says "noticeably faster" qualitatively. |
| 60-70% software adoption via peer recommendation | earlier research | No survey of newly-acquired customers found across any of the 5+ PMS vendors. this site says "peer recommendation is significant" qualitatively. |
| Solo CA earning ₹15 lakh/year average | earlier research | ICAI publishes only "Minimum Recommended Fees"; no member-income survey found. this site uses the band only directionally. |
| MOU "5K-10K registrations" per ICAI partnership | earlier research | No precedent data from any of 6+ ICAI-MOU vendors (SimplifyPractice, Papilio, Vidur, Zoho, etc.) located. this site flags as continuous monitoring. |
| GSTPs total enrolled count | industry assumption | GSTN exposes a "Locate a GSTP" search but no aggregate count. RTI required. |

## Read the rating, then read the cell

Three rules of thumb for using ratings on this site:

1. **High → quote freely.** A High-confidence number is investor-ready; an external pitch can cite it without disclaimer.
2. **Med → name the aggregator.** "Per Inc42, Tally FY25 revenue ₹710 cr" — not "Tally FY25 revenue ₹710 cr". The hop is small but transparency is large.
3. **Low / Unverified → never load-bear.** Use Low to set ceilings or contextualise; never to size a TAM, set a target, or anchor a phase trigger. Unverified claims should not appear in pitch materials at all.

The [data atlas](/caos/method/data-atlas/) is the ground truth. Every cell is rated. The rating is not decorative — it is the contract this site makes with the reader.
