The original CAOS plan sized one SKU for one buyer — the [1,00,138 CA firms](https://thefinancestory.com/mid-sized-indian-ca-firms-employing-20-percent-audit-workforce). That SKU is correct for Phase 0-3 but caps total addressable revenue at the CA-only band. The verified-numbers re-pass identifies two more buyers running on the same [15 atomic primitives](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/): in-house finance teams in [18.17 lakh active MCA companies](https://www.newkerala.com/news/o/number-active-companies-registered-india-crosses-1817-lakh-jan-297) and Audit Workspace consumers in the [326 of 483 Big-6-audited Nifty 500 companies](http://www.primedatabasegroup.com/newsroom/BIG_6_CONTINUE_DOMINANCE_OF_INDIAN_AUDIT_LANDSCAPE_2024-25.pdf).

The packaging answer is **one brand, three SKUs**, not three products and not three brands. The architectural overlap is 80%+; a dual-brand split would waste engineering reuse and confuse the marketing story; and **60.8% of the corporate buyer is, statistically, a CA herself** ([2,48,072 ICAI members in industry](https://finance.careers360.com/articles/icai-student-and-member-report-2025), ) — "CA-grade" reads as positive credibility transfer, not as a small-business toy. See [decision D2](/caos/decisions/d2-single-brand/) for the brand-strategy reasoning in full.

<Aside type="tip" title="The single-SKU rule">
A feature only earns multi-SKU placement if it serves at least one CA persona AND at least one finance-team persona — the architecture decision check, the one-line decision check for every new module proposed.
</Aside>

## SKU 1 — CAOS for Practice

The CA wedge. The current Phase 0-3 product. Sold to the [1,59,557 COP holders](https://finance.careers360.com/articles/icai-student-and-member-report-2025) practising in the [1,00,138 ICAI-registered firms](https://thefinancestory.com/mid-sized-indian-ca-firms-employing-20-percent-audit-workforce) — 72.6% sole-prop, 21,750 two-to-three-partner firms, 3,563 four-to-five-partner firms, and the 2,129 six-plus-partner firms that employ 41,478 staff (22.6% of total firm workforce).

**Core modules** (Phase 0-3 plan):

| Module | Powers | Source primitive |
|---|---|---|
| Client management | CA's client master, CRM, scope tracking | P2, P12 |
| Compliance calendar | 22-entity × ~100-deadline cascading matrix | P3 |
| Document collection | OTP-gated portal + WhatsApp + email | P4, P10 |
| Document intelligence (Tier 1) | 26AS, Form 16, bank statement, GSTR-9C, Tally TB | P1, P14 |
| Tally export | Idempotent voucher write-back | P9 |
| Billing | Engagement-based invoicing | P11 |
| Comms | Email + WhatsApp + portal abstracted | P10 |
| Client portal | OTP-gated public surface for client uploads | P4, P10, P11 |

**Pricing:** per-firm + per-staff seat, **₹500-1,500/seat/month**, 5-seat minimum. This matches CA mental model — CA firms already think in headcount-per-staff for engagement profitability) and benchmark against [Tally per-license ₹18K/yr](https://tallysolutions.com/about-tally/) and [Vyapar ₹2K-3K/month tiers](https://vyaparapp.in/desktop-pricing) for adjacent software spend.

**Add-ons:** WhatsApp messaging volume (per-1000 messages), document-AI extraction volume (per-1000 pages), portal storage (per-GB above default).

**Onboarding:** ICAI membership ID + email + first-5-clients import. Demo data on by default. The first-5-clients flow is the magic-moment rehearsal for the [Phase 1 CA-Led flywheel](/caos/flywheel/phase-1-ca-led/) — if the CA cannot get the first invite-and-upload round-trip done in under 2 minutes, the loop is dead.

## Why three SKUs, not one

The temptation is to ship a single tier-priced product and let buyers self-select. Three forces argue against that:

1. **The pricing unit differs by buyer.** A CA firm budgets per-seat per-month against engagement profit-per-staff. An in-house finance team budgets per-entity per-month against the compliance-software budget line. An enterprise budgets per-engagement annually against advisory + assurance + tooling. A single price model fits one buyer well and the other two badly. (See [pricing architecture](/caos/business-model/pricing-architecture/) for why per-volume + per-entity beats per-seat for compliance products.)

2. **The procurement gate differs by buyer.** A CA pays from a firm credit card on Day 1. A controller signs an MSA after 30-90 days. An enterprise CIO signs after a SOC 2 + ISO 27001 + security review that takes 6-18 months. Same product, three procurement paths — the SKU package shapes the path.

3. **The magic moment differs by buyer.** Practice's magic moment is "client uploads a doc in under 2 minutes." Business's is "vendor onboarded in one click instead of one hour." Enterprise's is "200 PBC items land as 200 tasks and the auditor sees real-time status." Each magic moment needs its own onboarding flow, its own free-trial scope, its own success metric. Three SKUs is the cleanest carrier for three magic moments.

## Why three SKUs, not three brands

Two further forces argue that three SKUs under one brand beats three brands:

1. **80%+ architectural overlap.** All three SKUs run on the same [15 atomic primitives](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/), the same document-intelligence pipeline, the same multi-entity data model. A dual or triple brand wastes the reuse signal — both internally (engineering team morale) and externally (buyer credibility about depth).

2. **The CA → client referral is a free distribution channel** — and it only works under one brand. "Ask your CA for CAOS" / "Your CA recommended CAOS for Business" reads naturally; "Ask your CA for CAOS and then sign up for FinanceFabric" does not. The [CA-referral channel](/caos/gtm/ca-referral/) needs the same brand on both sides.

The single-brand decision sits at [decision D2](/caos/decisions/d2-single-brand/). Revisit at ₹500 cr ARR.
