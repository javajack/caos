The unit economics question is not "what is the LTV/CAC?" — it is "which combinations of buyer × channel × SKU produce a defensible LTV/CAC, and which produce a sub-3x ratio that should not be funded?" The answer requires three inputs: WTP by persona, CAC by channel, and LTV grounded in retention assumptions reasonable for Indian B2B SaaS. The three combine into per-deal margin bands that bound which deals to chase and which to walk away from.

<Aside type="caution" title="The 33.8x anomaly">
a tactical framing quoted a 33.8x LTV/CAC for the original CA-only SKU. The author flagged this as anomalous in the source itself — it depended on a 5-year retention assumption with no churn, an artificially low CAC (CA-referral only), and an ARPU at the top of the band. The realistic normalized range is **8-12x** at maturity, with Year 1-2 ratios likely in the 3-6x band as channel mix matures. Never quote 33.8x externally. Lead with 8-12x and walk through the math.
</Aside>

## LTV math by SKU

LTV = ARPU × Gross Margin × Years Retained. Each input has a defensible band:

### CAOS for Practice — solo CA

- **ARPU**: ₹500-1,500/seat × 5 seats × 12 = **₹30K-90K/year**, midpoint ₹45K.
- **Gross margin**: 70-85% at scale (LLM cost the main variable; CA SKU has lower extraction volume per firm than Business).
- **Years retained**: 3-5 years (CA software switching is rare once Tally + PMS are wired; verified by Vyapar / Tally retention norms).
- **LTV** (midpoint): ₹45K × 0.78 × 4 = **₹1.4 lakh**

### CAOS for Business — lower-mid (₹50-200 cr revenue)

- **ARPU**: midpoint of ₹50K-1.5L/month band = ₹1L/month = **₹12 lakh/year**.
- **Gross margin**: 65-75% (LLM cost higher per-seat; AI extraction volume larger).
- **Years retained**: 2-4 years initially; rising to 4-6 years as the vendor-network compounds and switching cost grows.
- **LTV** (midpoint): ₹12L × 0.70 × 3.5 = **₹29.4 lakh**

### CAOS Enterprise — listed mid-cap

- **ARPU**: midpoint of ₹3-15L/month band = ₹9L/month = **₹1.08 cr/year**.
- **Gross margin**: 70-80% (LLM cost spreads over more entities; SOC 2 + dedicated CSM amortise across larger contracts).
- **Years retained**: 5-7 years (enterprise procurement cycles + SOC 2 audit-trail dependency = high switching cost).
- **LTV** (midpoint): ₹1.08 cr × 0.75 × 6 = **₹4.86 cr**

<Aside type="caution" title="Why the high ratios are misleading">
The CA-referral and audit-firm-partnership channels look implausibly attractive in the per-deal grid because the CAC excludes the channel-investment cost (building the CA-referral compensation engine, the 12-18 month Big-6 partnership development cycle). At the **portfolio level**, blending all channels with realistic mix and including channel-investment overhead, the normalized LTV/CAC lands in the **8-12x range** — strong for India B2B SaaS, but not the 33.8x or 100x+ that per-deal math suggests.
</Aside>

## Gross margin assumptions

Three cost layers determine gross margin:

1. **LLM cost (variable)** — the biggest scaling cost. CAOS's [LLM provider abstraction (design decision)](/caos/decisions/architecture-llm-routing/) and [cache-aware routing](/caos/product/document-intelligence/) keep this in the **15-25% of revenue band** at scale. Without caching it would be 35-50% of revenue and gross margin collapses to 50%.
2. **Cloud infrastructure (semi-variable)** — DB + storage + workers + comms. **5-10% of revenue** at scale; higher in early phases when the customer base is small.
3. **Channel rev-share (variable to channel)** — 15-20% of first-year ARR for CA-referral; 30% for Tally partner; 0% for direct PLG. Blended at portfolio level **8-15%**.

**Net gross margin band: 65-80%** depending on channel mix and SKU. Enterprise sits at the top of the band (lower per-rupee LLM cost; SOC 2 + dedicated CSM amortise); Business mid-band; Practice variable depending on extraction volume.

## The cost of enterprise — SOC 2 + ISO 27001 + DPDP

The single biggest line item that doesn't show up in per-deal economics is the compliance package required to clear enterprise procurement. From [risk #2](/caos/risks/compliance-cost/):

| Certification | Upfront cost | Ongoing cost/year | Required for | When to start |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **SOC 2 Type I** | ₹15-25 lakh | ₹5-8 lakh | Mid-market enterprise (₹500 cr-2,000 cr) | Y2 (Months 13-18) |
| **SOC 2 Type II** | ₹15-25 lakh additional | ₹8-12 lakh | Big-6 audit firm partnerships; listed-co enterprise | Y3 (Months 25-36) |
| **ISO 27001 framework + cert** | ₹15-25 lakh | ₹5-8 lakh | Listed-co enterprise; PSU; BFSI | Y3 |
| **DPDP DPA template + DPO** | ₹5-10 lakh | ₹2-5 lakh | All Business + Enterprise after [DPDP enforcement May 2027](https://www.meity.gov.in/data-protection-framework) | Y2-Y3 prep |
| **VPC-isolated tenant build** | ₹10-20 lakh | ₹3-6 lakh ongoing | BFSI / PSU clients (in lieu of on-prem) | Y3 |

**Total: ₹50-80 lakh upfront + ₹15-25 lakh/year ongoing.** This is the price of enterprise. It must be amortised across enterprise contracts; the per-deal LTV/CAC math above assumes this is paid at the company level and doesn't load into individual deal CAC. At 50 enterprise contracts × ₹1 cr ARPU = ₹50 cr ARR, the ₹25 lakh/year ongoing cost is **0.5% of revenue** — manageable. At 5 enterprise contracts × ₹1 cr = ₹5 cr ARR, it's 5% — painful. Hence the [hard rule](/caos/decisions/d3-sequencing/): **don't accept enterprise RFPs until at least 50 mid-market logos landed**, otherwise the compliance overhead crushes margin.

## Payback period

The CAC payback question — how many months before a deal pays back the acquisition cost — is the second-most-important unit-economics metric after LTV/CAC.

| SKU × channel | CAC | Monthly gross profit | Payback months |
|---|---|---|---|
| Practice (solo CA) — PLG | ₹10K | ₹3K (₹45K ARR × 78% × 1/12) | **3.4 months** |
| Practice (mid-firm) — PLG | ₹20K | ₹35K (₹5L ARR × 78% × 1/12) | **0.6 months** |
| Business (lower-mid) — CA-referral | ₹22K | ₹70K (₹12L ARR × 70% × 1/12) | **0.3 months** |
| Business (lower-mid) — Field sales | ₹3.5L | ₹70K | **5 months** |
| Business (upper-mid) — Field sales | ₹4L | ₹2.9L (₹50L ARR × 70% × 1/12) | **1.4 months** |
| Enterprise (listed) — Audit partnership | ₹2L | ₹6.75L (₹1.08 cr ARR × 75% × 1/12) | **0.3 months** |
| Enterprise (listed) — Field sales + SI | ₹10L | ₹6.75L | **1.5 months** |
| Enterprise (top-1000) — SI partnership | ₹20L | ₹6-9L | 2.2-3.3 months |

All payback periods sit under 6 months across the grid. This is the single most defensible unit-economics fact about the SKU + channel mix: even at conservative ARPU and conservative gross margin, no deal-shape requires more than half a year to pay back. CAC is recoverable; growth investment is reversible.

## What the 33.8x anomaly teaches

The original file 08 INSIGHT 4 number depends on three inputs that are not all true at once:
- 5-year retention with no churn (true at the top of the band only)
- CA-referral CAC of ₹5-15K (true for solo CA channel only)
- ARPU at the top of the WTP band (true for mid-firm-and-up only)

Combine them and you get 33.8x. Take any one of them off the top assumption and the ratio drops to 12-18x. Take two off and it drops to 6-10x. The **honest range** is 8-12x at maturity, with Year 1-2 ratios likely in the 3-6x band as channel mix matures and retention data accumulates. **Lead with 8-12x. Explain the math. Never quote 33.8x externally.**
