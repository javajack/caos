This is the master coverage map: **33 capabilities** mapped against **26 CA-PMS competitors + 5 enterprise-tier competitors**. Cells use `Y` (full coverage), `~` (partial / weak), and blank (none). The point of this page is not to score products against each other — that's done on the [per-product deep-dives](/caos/competitors/landscape/) — it is to show **where the entire field is empty**.

<Aside type="tip" title="How to read this matrix">
Read it column-down to evaluate one competitor's coverage. Read it row-across to evaluate how absent any one capability is from the entire field. The empty rows are the [white-space](/caos/competitors/feature-gaps/). The first 8-10 capabilities (CRM, calendar, tasks, time/billing, DMS) are saturated; everything past that thins out fast.
</Aside>

## The 33 capabilities mapped

For every cell that says `Y`, the source is a vendor disclosure on the [per-product deep-dive](/caos/competitors/landscape/) (not external assertion).

## What this view reveals

<Aside type="caution" title="The empty rows are the moat">
Three capabilities show **0/31 coverage** — litigation tracker, fixed assets register, transfer pricing documentation. Eight more show **1/31** — DSC inventory, audit working-paper management, sample-based testing, IFC, manager review, IND-AS Schedule III, XBRL, FEMA. These are the highest-leverage white-space targets for the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/).
</Aside>

### Observation 1 — practice operations is fully saturated

Client CRM, time/billing, task management, DMS, and mobile app are at 19-31/31 coverage. **Trying to win on these capabilities is impossible** — the field has 26 functioning products. The Phase 1 wedge has to win on a different dimension (WhatsApp-AI handoff, document intelligence wired into workflow, OTP-portal client experience), not on raw practice-ops feature parity.

### Observation 2 — document intelligence is concentrated, not absent

Doc OCR + Tally write-back has 9-13/31 coverage. **The differentiator is the integration with workflow**, not the OCR itself. VouchrIT and Digital Doc Sys exist as point solutions; what's missing is OCR feeding directly into the compliance calendar + reconciliation engine + notice inbox.

### Observation 3 — compliance is uneven, not unified

GST has 7-9/31 coverage. ROC has 3-4/31. TDS has 4-7/31. **No single product covers the full Indian compliance surface**. Even Bizalys (claimed 600+ workflow templates) is patchy. Masters India + Cygnet.One own the GSP-grade infrastructure but don't bridge into CA workflow.

### Observation 4 — audit-grade workflow is **functionally empty for CA-PMS**

The 5 audit-grade rows (audit working-papers, sample testing, IFC, manager review, IND-AS Schedule III) are all `0/26` for CA-PMS players. TeamMate (Wolters Kluwer) and MetricStream cover the enterprise side, but neither bridges to the Indian CA's tax-audit + 3CA/3CB + UDIN-bearing workflow. **This is the [Phase 3 Audit Workspace](/caos/flywheel/composite/) wedge** — the candidate flywheel that has zero direct competitor.

### Observation 5 — notice handling is a one-vendor field

Notice intake → triage → reply → outcome shows up only in Vaive AI (litigation co-pilot, partial coverage of GST notices). Litigation tracker is `0/31`. The [4.2 lakh+ GST notices issued FY22-23 alone](https://a2ztaxcorp.net/gst-8-years-report-released-gstn-highlights-record-revenue-compliance-milestones/), plus the IT department's 11.5 cr PAN deactivations creating downstream notice volume, is a **largely-unaddressed pain spectrum**.

### Observation 6 — corporate-card + AP is owned by the enterprise tier, not CA-PMS

Volopay, EnKash, Happay (now MakeMyTrip-owned) and Avalara cover this cluster well. **Zero CA-PMS player ships corporate-card or AP automation natively**. This is the [Phase 2 vendor-network](/caos/flywheel/composite/) wedge — vendor onboarding + master + reconciliation + payment is the right place to bridge the CA + enterprise sides.

### Observation 7 — cross-border and specialty (FEMA, TP, IND-AS, BRSR, XBRL) are essentially missing

Only IRIS Business Services covers BRSR / XBRL / IND-AS at any depth, and IRIS is enterprise-only. **No CA-PMS product** ships these. Yet [SEBI's BRSR Core mandate hits top 250 listed cos by FY26 and top 1,000 by FY27](http://www.primedatabasegroup.com/newsroom/BIG_6_CONTINUE_DOMINANCE_OF_INDIAN_AUDIT_LANDSCAPE_2024-25.pdf) — and the auditor is the Big-6 + mid-tier, who need a workspace that talks to the in-house team. This is the [Phase 5+ specialty expansion](/caos/flywheel/composite/) zone.

## Where the matrix sends the product roadmap

| Insight from matrix | Implication |
|---|---|
| Practice ops saturated | Don't compete on parity. Win on document intelligence × WhatsApp × OTP-portal handoff. |
| Doc-intel concentrated | OCR alone is not the moat — the integration into calendar + reco + notice is. Build OCR as a Phase 1 primitive, not a feature. |
| Notice-handling near-empty | Notice Inbox is a Phase 2 wedge. Vaive AI is the only co-pilot; CAOS can ship the full lifecycle (intake → reply → outcome). |
| Audit-grade `0/26` for CA-PMS | Phase 3 Audit Workspace bridge is greenfield. NFRA's [94 disciplinary orders + 12 inspection reports](https://taxguru.in/company-law/action-nfra-prevent-audit-failure-regulatory-lapses.html) creates tooling-grade demand. |
| Vendor-network / AP owned by enterprise tier | Phase 2 vendor-network is the bridge wedge. Combine CA-side trust + enterprise-side AP automation. |
| BRSR / XBRL / FEMA / TP `0-1/31` | Phase 5+ specialty expansion. Each one is a 6-12 month build with no direct CA-PMS competitor. |
| Corporate-card + AP `0/26` for CA-PMS | Don't compete with Volopay / EnKash on cards. Integrate as a partner; own the workflow layer above. |
