The single most important sentence in the entire CAOS planning corpus is the negative one. CAOS is **not** a CA practice management SaaS, **not** a Tally replacement, **not** an ERP, **not** a general ledger, **not** a payroll engine, **not** an IFRS reporting engine, **not** an inventory accounting system. It is the **document, intelligence, workflow, and compliance fabric for Indian financial paperwork** — anchored to PAN/GSTIN/CIN identity, designed for the specific shape of Indian regulation, and tuned to the messy edges that ERPs do not own.

Every product decision in this section flows from that one sentence. If a feature drifts toward replacing the ERP, replacing the GL, computing payroll, or generating IFRS-compliant statements, the feature is mis-scoped. If a feature collects, classifies, extracts, reconciles, or routes a financial document, the feature belongs.

## What CAOS IS — five verbs

The product surface area is exhausted by five verbs. Every screen, every API endpoint, every worker job is one of these.

| # | Verb | What it means in the pipeline | Why ERPs leak this |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Capture** | Pull unstructured documents from any of four channels: web upload, mobile camera, WhatsApp/email forward, OTP-gated public portal | ERPs assume structured input; they do not ingest a PDF screenshot or a WhatsApp-forwarded vendor invoice |
| 2 | **Classify** | Identify what each document is via 4-stage gate: filename heuristics → content patterns → optional OCR → LLM classification | ERPs do not classify; they require the human to declare doctype on entry |
| 3 | **Extract** | Pull structured JSON via per-doctype LLM pipelines with cache-aware routing, validation retry, and per-client mapping memory | ERPs do not extract; they expect typed input. Add-ons that try are India-blind. |
| 4 | **Reconcile** | Match `ledger ↔ external source` (GSTR-2B vs purchase register, 26AS vs books, bank stmt vs Tally, vendor SOA vs AP) with confidence scoring | ERPs do this badly across statutes/portals; ClearTax breaks past 10 GSTINs. |
| 5 | **Route** | Send the right artefact to the right human / system: queue for CA review, push voucher to Tally with idempotent REMOTEID, dispatch reminder, escalate notice | ERPs assume one-actor workflow; CAOS supports maker-checker, multi-org, multi-channel |

The architecture diagram for the CAOS backend (`/home/rakesh/work/CAOS/.digest/rebuild-knowledge-map.md` §5) is literally these five verbs wired into one Go service: an inbox + 4-stage classifier feeds a doc-intelligence pipeline that emits a ReviewItem; the CA approves; a Tally export job runs; metering rolls up; the loop closes.

## What CAOS is NOT — five disqualifying claims

Every one of these has been rejected at the architectural level. Most are listed as the "Fundamental rearchitecture" set in the [25 finance-team use cases analysis](/caos/product/use-cases/). They sit outside the product surface forever, not just at launch.

| # | What CAOS is NOT | Why we will not build it | What you use instead |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **A replacement for Tally / SAP / Oracle / Zoho Books** | The ERP is the GL of record. Re-implementing what Tally does forks from the Tally-XML thesis and destroys the moat. | Keep your existing GL; CAOS plugs in via [Tally / ERP integration](/caos/product/tally-erp-integration/) |
| 2 | **A general ledger / double-entry posting engine** | No double-entry GL inside CAOS; vouchers are written to Tally with idempotent REMOTEID. Cabuddy holds Tally master mirrors only. | Tally / SAP / Oracle / Zoho holds the books |
| 3 | **A payroll computation engine** | Only payroll documents are classified (Form 16, salary register). No payroll-run engine, no PT/PF/ESI computation. | greytHR, Zoho Payroll, Keka, RazorpayX Payroll |
| 4 | **An inventory accounting system** | Tally master mirrors only; no FIFO/LIFO/weighted-avg, no lot/serial transactional engine. | Tally Inventory, Unicommerce, Zoho Inventory |
| 5 | **An IFRS / IND-AS reporting engine** | No parallel reporting engine, no IND-AS taxonomy, no consolidation eliminations. BS/P&L/CF are T6 OUTPUT classify-only docs. | OneSource, IRIS Carbon (XBRL), HFM (consolidation) |

The exclusion list is also where competitive positioning gets honest. The [TAM in the original CA-only frame](/caos/market/tam/) was capped at ₹141-364 cr; the [verified finance-team-fabric TAM](/caos/flywheel/composite/) is 5-10x larger. But we get to that bigger TAM precisely *because* we refuse to compete with SAP / Oracle / Workday / IBM Cognos. The product position is "the messy edges and the document fabric," not "ERP replacement." Honour that, and the moat compounds; violate it, and you join the graveyard of mid-market Indian ERP wannabes.

## The architecture test for any new feature

Every feature proposal — new module, new doctype, new workflow, new partnership — runs through a six-question gate before it can enter the roadmap. Five of the six come from the [atomic primitives architecture test](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/); the sixth is the "messy edges" check.

:::caution[Architecture test for a new feature]
1. **Primitive composition.** Does the feature decompose into one or two of the [15 atomic primitives](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/) plus a presentation layer? If no — the primitive list is incomplete or the feature is mis-scoped. Either fix the list (rare) or kill the feature (common).
2. **Dual-persona test.** Does it serve at least one CA persona AND at least one finance-team persona? If only one — label clearly; check that the single segment is large enough; consider whether the design is accidentally locking in a CA-only assumption (per-client pricing, ICAI ID as identity, "client" as the master noun).
3. **ERP-non-replacement.** Does it work on top of Tally / SAP / Oracle / Zoho without replacing them? If the feature requires CAOS to become the GL, drop it.
4. **Architecture cost check.** Does it require new architecture from the "[Fundamental rearchitecture](/caos/product/use-cases/)" list (multi-entity consol, IFRS engine, payroll computation, FX revaluation, real-time GL)? If yes, write a new design decisions and estimate the months of engineering. Most should die at this gate.
5. **Use-case ranking.** Is it in the [top 25 finance use cases or 7 bridge use cases](/caos/product/use-cases/)? Or is it a CA-must-have in the CAOS plan? If none — why are we building it?
6. **Messy-edges test.** Would Tally / SAP / Oracle / Zoho own this in 5 years? If yes, we should not build it; that's their core surface and they will compete us out. If no, the moat is durable.
:::

The test exists because the most common product-strategy failure in Indian B2B fintech is feature-creep into adjacent system territory. The team that built the CA wedge will be tempted, in months 18-24, to ship "just a little payroll" or "just a basic GL" because customers ask for it. Customers always ask for it. Saying yes destroys the moat. The architecture test is the discipline that lets you say no with a reason — not "we ran out of time" but "this fails Question 3 and Question 6."
