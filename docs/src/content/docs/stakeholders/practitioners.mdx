The Indian compliance practitioner economy is regulated by **five statutory bodies**, totalling approximately **5.95 lakh organised professionals**, with another **10 lakh CA students** in the pipeline. Each body owns a distinct workflow surface — but they overlap heavily on document creation and consumption, and a single Indian company typically engages four of the five (CA + CS + CMA + Valuer) for routine compliance.

The CA wedge is the densest entry point because (a) the CA serves the broadest workflow surface, (b) the CA's referral has the highest peer-network velocity, and (c) the CA's client portal is the [Trojan horse](/caos/flywheel/phase-1-ca-led/) into the in-house finance team. But every primitive that serves the CA also serves a CS / CMA / Valuer / IP — the [atomic primitives](/caos/product/atomic-primitives/) are universal across the practitioner classes.

<Aside type="tip" title="Why list all five bodies, not just CA">
verified each body separately. The cross-stakeholder document flow map shows that 8 of the 30+ document families are co-owned by CA + CS + CMA + Valuer + IP. The Audit Workspace bridge (Phase 3) and the Specialty workflow expansion (Phase 5+) need explicit data models for all five.
</Aside>

### What practising CAs own (workflow surface)

A practising CA firm typically delivers some subset of the following. The breadth depends on partner-count and specialisation; a 2-3 partner firm picks 4-6 of these, a 6+ partner firm covers most.

- **Statutory audit** under Companies Act 2013 (sec 139-148) — 3CA-CD / 3CB-CD; signed with UDIN
- **Tax audit** under Income Tax Act sec 44AB — 3CA-CD / 3CB-CD; the 29.5 lakh tax audits filed by Sep 30, 2023 ([TaxCorner / CBDT](https://www.taxcorner.co.in/2023/10/cbdt-releases-tax-audit-reports-filing-data-till-30-09-2023.html))
- **GST advisory and filings** — GSTR-1/3B/2B reco, GSTR-9/9C, RCM, ITC optimisation, notice handling
- **Income tax** — ITRs (corporate and individual), TDS returns (24Q/26Q/27Q/27EQ), TCS (27EQ), assessment representation
- **MCA / ROC** — AOC-4, MGT-7, board minutes, secretarial liaison (often jointly with CS)
- **Transfer pricing** — 3CEB, master file, local file, CbCR
- **Valuation** — under Companies Act, IT Act, FEMA, IBC, SEBI (overlap with IBBI Registered Valuers)
- **FEMA** — FC-GPR, FLA, ECB-2, ODI, Form 15CA/15CB
- **Insolvency professional services** — RP under IBC (overlap with IBBI IPs)
- **Cost audit liaison** — joint with CMA where applicable
- **Virtual CFO services** — 5-10K firms estimated as the fastest-growing segment

## ICSI — Company Secretaries

The Institute of Company Secretaries of India (ICSI) regulates ~75-79K members across 73 chapters. The CS workflow centres on **Companies Act 2013, SEBI LODR, FEMA, and IBC** — heavy on event-based filings and listed-company governance, lighter on transactional reconciliation.

### What practising CSs own (workflow surface)

- **MCA21 event filings** — DIR-3 KYC, DIR-12, MGT-14, CHG, MR-3 (secretarial audit), AOC-4 (joint with CA)
- **Board governance** — agenda, minutes, resolutions under SS-1/SS-2, statutory registers
- **Listed-company compliance** — SEBI LODR (Reg 30, 33), Reg 23 RPT, PIT Reg 7 SDD, Reg 24 financial results
- **DSC management** — physical token holding for clients' directors and authorised signatories
- **FEMA** — FC-GPR, FLA, ECB-2, ODI (overlap with CA)
- **IBC** — pre-admission filings, RP support (overlap with IPs)
- **Secretarial audit** — MR-3 with UDIN

## ICMAI — Cost and Management Accountants

The Institute of Cost Accountants of India (ICMAI) regulates ~98K active members across 117 chapters. The CMA workflow centres on **cost records under sec 148 of the Companies Act, MIS for manufacturing, GST cost-related issues, and government/PSU costing**.

### What practising CMAs own (workflow surface)

- **Cost audit** under sec 148 of the Companies Act — CRA-2 (appointment), CRA-3 (audit report), CRA-4 (filing); UDIN-signed
- **Cost records maintenance** — for the ~30 industries notified under Cost Audit Rules
- **MIS and management accounts** — segment reporting, contribution analysis, costing for inventory valuation
- **Government and PSU costing** — tariff filings, regulatory cost statements (electricity, telecom, drugs)
- **GST cost-related issues** — RCM cost recovery, ITC apportionment, valuation rules
- **Valuation** — limited overlap with Registered Valuers; mostly Plant & Machinery class
- **IBC** — RP role through ICAI-CMA IPA (one of the three IPAs)

## IBBI — Registered Valuers

Registered Valuers were established by the IBBI under the Companies (Registered Valuers and Valuation) Rules, 2017. There are **6,144 registered valuers** as of August 2025, organised across three asset classes — Land & Building (L&B), Plant & Machinery (P&M), and Securities or Financial Assets (SFA) ([IBBI service-provider page](https://ibbi.gov.in/service-provider/rvs)).

| Metric | Value | As of | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Registered Valuers** | **6,144** | Aug 2025 | [IBBI service-provider page](https://ibbi.gov.in/service-provider/rvs) |
| Asset classes | 3 (L&B / P&M / SFA) | — | [IBBI](https://ibbi.gov.in/service-provider/rvs) |

### What Registered Valuers own (workflow surface)

- **Valuation under Companies Act 2013** — sec 247 mandates registered valuer for fresh issue, transfer, restructuring, ESOP
- **Valuation under IT Act** — sec 56(2)(viib) Rule 11UA share valuation, capital gains
- **Valuation under FEMA** — pricing guidelines for non-resident transactions
- **Valuation under IBC** — fair value and liquidation value for CIRP
- **Valuation under SEBI** — preferential issue, takeover, delisting
- **Valuation reports** — UDIN-signed; filed alongside the corresponding statutory action

## IBBI — Insolvency Professionals

Insolvency Professionals (IPs) administer the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code 2016. **There are 4,527 IPs registered, but only 49% (2,198) hold a valid Authorisation for Assignment (AFA)** — the operative gating mechanism per the IBBI rules ([Business Standard, Aug 2025](https://www.business-standard.com/industry/news/ibbi-proposes-assignment-cap-for-insolvency-professionals-to-cut-delays-125081301524_1.html)).

### What IPs own (workflow surface)

- **CIRP** (Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process) — Form FA, Form B, claims register, CoC management, IM, RP, voting
- **Liquidation** — when CIRP fails; asset sale and distribution
- **Personal Insolvency** under IBC (for guarantors of corporate debt)
- **Pre-pack insolvency** for MSMEs (post-2021 amendment)
- **Resolution Plan negotiation** with CoC and resolution applicants
- **NCLT representation** — joint with counsel

The combination of (a) a low AFA-valid pool, (b) high settlement volume (30,000+ pre-admission), and (c) IBBI's published assignment-cap proposal makes IP workflow tooling a focused specialty surface — small TAM but high willingness to pay per active engagement.

## Why the practitioner pool is the entry wedge
