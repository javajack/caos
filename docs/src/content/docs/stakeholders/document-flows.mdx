This is the **substrate of the flywheel**. Every cell where two stakeholders share a document is a potential bilateral edge in the network graph. Every edge is a friction point today and an opportunity for CAOS to own the handoff tomorrow.

The 30+ documents below are grouped into **seven families** — GST, Income Tax, MCA / corporate law, Bank / Treasury / FEMA, Audit, Litigation, and Specialty. For each document, the table records:

- **Created by** — which stakeholder class produces the document
- **Consumed by** — which stakeholder class(es) read or reconcile against it
- **Certified by** — which stakeholder signs / authenticates / attests
- **Archive owner** — where the canonical copy lives
- **Cross-stakeholder edges** — the actual handoff path

<Aside type="tip" title="Why this inventory matters">
demonstrates that the friction in Indian compliance is not document creation — that is the easy part, mostly automated by the GSTN / IT portal / MCA21 / RBI portals already. The friction is the **boundary handoffs** between stakeholder classes. A platform that owns the handoffs owns the workflow. This is the substrate of every [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) candidate.
</Aside>

## GST family

GST is the densest document family. The combination of e-invoice + e-way bill + GSTR-1 / 2A / 2B / 3B / 9 / 9C creates a tightly-coupled flow that crosses vendor, IRP, GSTN, enterprise, CA, and counterparty CA boundaries — with cross-side reconciliation expected at multiple stages.

| Document | Created by | Consumed by | Certified by | Archive owner | Cross-stakeholder edges |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tax invoice (B2B)** | S11 (vendor) | S10 (enterprise AP) → S5/S6 (cost), S9 (audit) | S2 / S3 (signatory); IRP for IRN | S10 (Tally / SAP) | Vendor → Enterprise → CA → Auditor |
| **E-invoice (with IRN)** | S11 + IRP | GSTN, S10 AP, S12 customer | IRP system | GSTN + IRP + S10 | Vendor → IRP → GSTN → CAOS → CA |
| **Customer invoice (AR)** | S10 (enterprise) | S12 (customer) | S2 / signatory | S10 | Enterprise → Customer → their AP → their CA |
| **E-way bill (EWB)** | S10 / transporter | GSTN, RTO, S12 customer | EWB portal | GSTN | Enterprise → EWB portal → Transporter → Recipient |
| **GSTR-1 (outward supplies return)** | S10 / S1 (CA filing for client) | GSTN, S12 customer (auto-flow to 2A/2B) | S1 / S2 | GSTN | Enterprise → CA → GSTN → Counterparty's CA |
| **GSTR-2A (auto-populated inward)** | GSTN (auto from suppliers' 1) | S10, S1, S2 | GSTN | GSTN | GSTN → CA / Enterprise (reco against PR) |
| **GSTR-2B (static auto-populated for ITC)** | GSTN (auto monthly) | S10, S1, S2 | GSTN | GSTN | GSTN → CA / Enterprise (reco against PR) |
| **GSTR-3B (monthly summary return)** | S10 / S1 | GSTN | S1 / S2 | GSTN | Enterprise → CA → GSTN |
| **GSTR-9 (annual return)** | S10 / S1 | GSTN | S1 / S2 | GSTN | Enterprise → CA → GSTN |
| **GSTR-9C (reconciliation statement & certification)** | S10 / S1 (CA-certified) | GSTN | S1 (signed with UDIN) + S2 | GSTN | Enterprise → CA → UDIN → GSTN |
| **RCM (Reverse Charge Mechanism) self-invoice** | S10 (recipient under RCM) | GSTN | S2 | GSTN + S10 | Enterprise → Self-invoice → GST 3B / 9 → CA |
| **Credit note / Debit note** | S10 / S11 | S10 / S11 (counter side), GSTN | S2 / signatory | GSTN + S10 | Vendor or Customer → Other side → GSTN auto-link |

## Income Tax family

The IT document family is tightly coupled with TDS and tax-audit. The key cross-stakeholder property: **Form 26AS / AIS / TIS** is consumed by both the taxpayer's CA (for ITR prep) and the recipient (for verifying TDS deducted), creating a natural reconciliation edge.

## The pattern — why boundary handoffs are the moat

Every document above crosses **at least two stakeholder boundaries**. Many cross four or five:

- A **GST tax invoice** is created by the vendor (S11), routed through the IRP (S13) and GSTN, consumed by the recipient enterprise (S10), processed by their AP team, validated against GSTR-2B by their GST Lead (F3), reconciled by their CA (S1), and verified by their auditor (S9) at year-end. **Six stakeholder boundaries crossed by one document.**

- A **statutory audit report** is created by the auditor (S9), based on documents from the in-house team (S2 + S10) and certified work papers from the engagement CA (S1). It is consumed by shareholders, the MCA21 portal (regulator), the audit committee (S2 + board), the lender (S14 for covenant monitoring), and the public (listed-co disclosure). **Five stakeholder boundaries.**

- A **vendor master + KYC** entry crosses the vendor (S11), procurement (S10 ops), AP team (S10 ops), Controller (F2), Direct Tax Lead (F4 for 194Q tracking), AP Manager (F5 for MSME 45-day tracking), AR team of the vendor (S11 again, mirror side), and ultimately the Phase 4 institutional capital pool (S14) for credit underwriting. **Eight stakeholder boundaries** (across both sides of the relationship).

The friction in Indian compliance is not document **creation** — most documents above are auto-generated, auto-routed, auto-stored. The friction is the **boundary handoffs** between stakeholder classes. Every handoff today involves email, WhatsApp, a courier, a portal-login, a download, a re-upload, an Excel template, a confirmation call.

**A platform that owns the handoffs owns the workflow.**

This is the substrate of every flywheel candidate in the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/):

- **Phase 1** owns the CA-to-client handoff (the practitioner client portal)
- **Phase 2** owns the enterprise-to-vendor handoff (the vendor onboarding + verification flow)
- **Phase 3** owns the auditor-to-liaison-to-CA handoff (the Audit Workspace shared evidence locker)
- **Phase 4** owns the entity-to-institutional-consumer handoff (consent-mediated graph access via AA)

Each phase compounds because the handoffs accumulate persistent state: vendor profiles validated once become free verifications for every next enterprise; PBC items resolved once become precedent for next year's audit; consent flows established once become repeat-use lender data feeds.
