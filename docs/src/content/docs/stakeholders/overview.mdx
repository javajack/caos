Stakeholders are not "users" — they are **network nodes**. Each one shares documents and workflow with at least two others. That is the substrate of the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/): every entity that joins is simultaneously a buyer, a counterparty to other buyers, a regulated entity to its supervisors, and a data source for institutional consumers downstream.

The original CAOS research treated practitioners as the only relevant stakeholder. The verified-numbers re-pass identifies **14 distinct classes** — each with its own population, its own primary workflows, and its own bilateral edges to the others. Building for one class without designing the bilateral edges to its neighbours is what creates the document handoff friction that Indian compliance is famous for.

<Aside type="tip" title="Why 14, not 7">
collapses regulators and portals into a "cross-cutting" obligation rather than a stakeholder class. The 14 here are buyer-and-user classes — each one writes, reads, certifies, or archives at least one document on the [document flow map](/caos/stakeholders/document-flows/).
</Aside>

## The atomic primitives map

The most expensive moment in B2B SaaS is teaching a buyer how the product maps to their workflow. ## Document flows in one glance

Every cell where two stakeholders share a document is a potential bilateral edge in the network graph. The full inventory is on the [document flows page](/caos/stakeholders/document-flows/) — here is the headline map:

| Document family | Created by | Consumed by | Cross-stakeholder edges |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tax invoice / e-invoice | S11 (vendor) + IRP | S10 (enterprise AP), S12, S9 audit | Vendor → IRP → GSTN → CAOS → CA |
| GSTR-1 / 3B / 2B / 9 / 9C | S10 / S1 | GSTN, S12 customer (auto-flow to 2A/2B) | Enterprise → CA → GSTN → Counterparty's CA |
| Form 26AS / AIS / TIS | CBDT (auto) | Taxpayer + S1 + S2 | CBDT → CA → Reco against books |
| ITR + tax audit (3CA-CD/3CB-CD) | S10 / S1 | CBDT, S9 audit, S14 lender diligence | Enterprise → CA → CBDT → Lender |
| Statutory audit report | S9 | Shareholders, MCA, S14 | Auditor → Board → MCA → Stock exchange |
| AOC-4 / MGT-7 / DIR-3 KYC | S10 / S3 / S1 | MCA21, public | Enterprise → CS → MCA → Public |
| GST notice / IT notice / hearing pack | Govt | S10 + S1 + S2 | Govt → Enterprise → CA → Reply → Order |
| Vendor master + KYC | S10 procurement | S10 AP, S2 controller | Vendor → Procurement → AP → All filing systems |
| Bank statement | Bank | S10 treasury, S2 controller, S1 CA | Bank → AA → CAOS → CA / Enterprise |
| BRSR / BRSR Core | S10 BRSR lead | SEBI, S9 assurance | Plant → BRSR Lead → CSO → Auditor → SEBI |

**Pattern:** every document above crosses **at least two** stakeholder boundaries. The friction in Indian compliance is not document creation — it is the boundary handoffs. **A platform that owns the handoffs owns the workflow.** This is the substrate of every flywheel candidate in the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/).

## Why this matters for the flywheel

The atlas is not an inventory exercise. It is the proof that the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) compounds across **all four loops simultaneously** because every entity that joins is a node in every loop:

- A **CA who joins in Phase 1** brings 50-300 client entities — each one is a future S10 (operations side) and a future buyer of the [Business SKU](/caos/business-model/three-skus/).
- Each **client entity** has 50-200 vendors and customers — Phase 2's vendor-network compounds across the 9.92 lakh e-invoice generators and 81.88 lakh recipients.
- Each **listed client** is audited by one of the Big-6 (67% of Nifty 500) — Phase 3's Audit Workspace lands at a concentrated 326-logo pool.
- Every **GSTIN/PAN/CIN** in the system is a candidate AA-linked entity — Phase 4 monetises lender access to the consent-mediated graph through the 1,157 AA participants.

Read each stakeholder page to see the populations, the today's-hack, and the primitive that turns the class into a buyer. Then read the [composite flywheel](/caos/flywheel/composite/) to see how the buyer becomes a node, and the node becomes the moat.
