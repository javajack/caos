The Indian statutory audit market is **dramatically more concentrated than the original CAOS research described**. The Big-6 audit firms own **67% of Nifty 500 audits (326 of 483)** and **34% of NSE main board (694 of 2,069)** — a concentration far above what the file 03 competitor scan reflected ([PrimeDatabase 2024-25 report](http://www.primedatabasegroup.com/newsroom/BIG_6_CONTINUE_DOMINANCE_OF_INDIAN_AUDIT_LANDSCAPE_2024-25.pdf)).

Audit firms are simultaneously **stakeholders** (S9 in the [14-class atlas](/caos/stakeholders/overview/) — they certify documents that flow across CA, CS, in-house finance, and regulators) and **buyers** of the [Audit Workspace](/caos/flywheel/phase-3-audit-workspace/) — the Phase 3 bridge that compresses 4 weeks of email PBC chaos into one shared evidence locker.

<Aside type="tip" title="Why &quot;Big-6&quot;, not &quot;Big-4&quot;">
PrimeDatabase's 2024-25 audit landscape report formally tracks the Big-6 = Big-4 + Grant Thornton Bharat + BDO India. Both GT Bharat (12,000+ people, 224 partners) and BDO India (11,000+ headcount, 350+ partners) have crossed the threshold of being individually larger than the smallest Big-4 by India headcount. The concentration data is reported on the Big-6 basis throughout this page.
</Aside>

### The audit affiliates

The Big-4 do not perform statutory audit under their global names — they use Indian audit affiliates that are separate ICAI-registered firms. The two largest affiliates:

| Affiliate | Big-4 firm | Partners | Professionals | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **BSR & Co LLP** | KPMG (audit) | **120+** | **5,000+** | [bsr-co.in](https://bsr-co.in/Home/AboutUs) |
| **SR Batliboi & Co LLP** | EY (audit) | **125** | **3,500+** | [srbatliboi.in](https://www.srbatliboi.in/about-us) |

These affiliates are the actual sign-off entities for statutory audits and are bound by ICAI norms — including the **20-audits-per-partner cap** under Section 141(3) of the Companies Act 2013 ([Ebizfiling on company audit ceiling](https://ebizfiling.com/blog/company-audit-ceiling-limit-under-the-companies-act-2013/)). The cap excludes OPCs, dormant companies, and small companies, but it is the single regulatory constraint that limits how concentrated a single audit firm can become in the listed audit market.

This concentration matters because:

- **The lighthouse pool is small.** 326 Nifty 500 logos = the entire Phase 3 lighthouse target population. Landing 250 of these is 77% of the available pool.
- **The buyer is concentrated.** Six firms own 67% of the Nifty 500. Six successful pilots = market signal.
- **Each firm is multi-pilot capable.** EY's 1,000+ partners × 5 listed audits each ≈ 5,000 partner-audit engagements per year. Capturing 50 of those at one firm is 1% of one firm's engagement volume.

## NFRA — the regulatory tailwind

The National Financial Reporting Authority (NFRA) was constituted under sec 132 of the Companies Act 2013 to oversee audit quality and discipline auditors of public-interest entities. Its disciplinary record creates **structural demand for audit-tooling-grade documentation** — every working paper now has to survive NFRA inspection.

| NFRA metric | Value | As of | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cumulative disciplinary orders** | **94** | Early 2025 | [TaxGuru on NFRA](https://taxguru.in/company-law/action-nfra-prevent-audit-failure-regulatory-lapses.html) |
| **Audit firms penalised** | 19 | Early 2025 | [TaxGuru on NFRA](https://taxguru.in/company-law/action-nfra-prevent-audit-failure-regulatory-lapses.html) |
| **Individual CAs penalised** | 84 | Early 2025 | [TaxGuru on NFRA](https://taxguru.in/company-law/action-nfra-prevent-audit-failure-regulatory-lapses.html) |
| **Inspection reports published** | 12 | Since Dec 2022 | [TaxGuru on NFRA](https://taxguru.in/company-law/action-nfra-prevent-audit-failure-regulatory-lapses.html) |

NFRA inspections cover the largest audit firms first — meaning the Big-6 are continuously under regulatory scrutiny for the rigour of their working papers. This translates into a willingness to adopt tooling that produces NFRA-compatible structured documentation. **The Phase 3 Audit Workspace ships with an NFRA-2 compatible working-paper schema**) — making Big-6 adoption frictionless.

## The audit workflow surface (Phase 3 wedge)

A typical Big-6 statutory audit engagement runs the following workflow surface, all of which is today fragmented across Caseware (auditor side), Excel (client side), email (queries), and Drive (attachments):

| Audit workflow | Today's tool | CAOS primitive |
|---|---|---|
| **PBC (Provided By Client) list** | Excel template emailed to client | P4 (request workflow) — list lands as 200 tasks on liaison's CAOS |
| **Client queries log** | Email + Excel | P6 (notice / query lifecycle) |
| **Working paper preparation** | Caseware / firm-built tool | Workspace integrates with Caseware via export; no replacement |
| **Evidence storage** | Drive / SharePoint per engagement | P5 (evidence locker tied to filing) |
| **Maker-checker review** | Caseware / partner sign-off | P8 (maker-checker) — partner approval state machine |
| **Audit committee pack** | Manual PDF assembly | P14 (semi-structured form extraction) |
| **NFRA-compatible documentation** | Manual structured filing | P11 (compliance-grade audit trail) — NFRA-2 schema |
| **Subsequent engagement reuse** | Cold-start each year | Persistent evidence locker survives partner / staff turnover |

The wedge is **not** to replace Caseware on the auditor side. The wedge is to **own the client interface** — PBC, queries, attachments, supports — where Caseware breaks and where no incumbent exists today. The Audit Workspace + Caseware integration is the pitch.

## Pricing — verified Big-6 audit fees as the budget shadow

The Big-6 audit fee per listed client typically runs **₹70 lakh - ₹1 cr per audit per year**. Implication for the Audit Workspace pricing:

- ₹25-75K per engagement (auditor side) is **0.5-1% of the audit fee** — well within "tooling line item" budget
- ₹3-15L per listed client per year (client / in-house side) is **0.5-2% of the audit fee** — well within the "audit committee tooling" budget
- The combined per-engagement revenue (auditor + client) at ₹6 lakh per Nifty 500 client is the basis of the Phase 3 ARR projection: **750 listed × ₹6L = ₹45 cr ARR** plus mid-tier of **5,000 firm-engagements × ₹50K = ₹25 cr ARR** = **~₹70 cr from Phase 3 alone by Year 4**
